Why Memorization Matters

Lawsuits, privacy, the central question

The Central Question

Generator $f_\theta$ trained on corpus $\mathcal{D}$, sample $x \in \mathcal{D}$.

Can a query recover $x$ verbatim?

Memorization: training samples resurfacing through prompts.

Distinct from generalization. Distinct from membership inference.

Three Stakeholders

Privacy

PII, medical records, secrets.

Copyright

Books, code, news, images, characters.

Security

Canaries, credentials, system prompts.

Memorization is the empirical signature of all three risks.

Live Lawsuits (2023–2026)

  • NYT v. OpenAI — verbatim article reproduction
  • Authors Guild v. OpenAI — copyrighted books
  • Getty v. Stability AI — image regurgitation with watermark
  • Disney v. Midjourney — character likeness
  • Andersen v. Stability AI — class action over training art

Plaintiffs cite extracted reproductions as direct evidence.

Memorization in Diffusion

From extraction to sharpness of the probability landscape

Diffusion Recap

Forward kernel (variance preserving):

$$q_{t\mid 0}(x_t \mid x_0) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\!\big(\sqrt{\alpha_t}\,x_0,\;(1-\alpha_t) I\big).$$

Reverse SDE driven by the score:

$$dx_t \;=\; \big[f(x_t,t) - g^2(t)\,\underbrace{\nabla_{x_t}\!\log p_t(x_t)}_{s(x_t)}\big]\,dt + g(t)\,d\bar w_t.$$

Conditional score $s(x_t, c) = \nabla_{x_t} \log p_t(x_t \mid c)$ via classifier-free guidance.

Extracting Training Images (Carlini 2023)

First demonstration that diffusion models memorize verbatim.

  • Generate many samples per prompt under CFG
  • Cluster by tiled $\ell_2$ pixel distance across non-overlapping patches
  • Verify match against LAION training set

Hundreds of training images recovered from Stable Diffusion v1.4.

Carlini, Hayes, Nasr, Jagielski, Sehwag, Tramèr, Balle, Ippolito, Wallace, "Extracting Training Data from Diffusion Models", USENIX Security 2023.

Replication Detection (Somepalli 2023)

Quantify copy via SSCD self-supervised image descriptors.

  • Top-$k$ retrieval over training set per generated image
  • Threshold SSCD similarity for partial vs exact copy
  • Duplicated training images dominate replication

Mitigation: RTA / RNA — random token / number injection in prompts.

Somepalli, Singla, Goldblum, Geiping, Goldstein, "Diffusion Art or Digital Forgery? Investigating Data Replication in Diffusion Models", CVPR 2023.

Reproducible Extraction (Webster 2023)

White-box and black-box prompt search yielding 500 memorized prompts for SD v1.4.

  • Edge-and-noise statistics flag candidate generations
  • Prompt set is the de facto benchmark for diffusion memorization
  • 219 additional prompts released for SD v2.0
Webster, "A Reproducible Extraction of Training Images from Diffusion Models", arXiv 2305.08694, 2023.

Score-Norm Metric (Wen 2024)

Memorized prompts steer generation hard via classifier-free guidance.

$$\big\| s_\theta^\Delta(x_t) \big\| \;:=\; \big\| s_\theta(x_t, c) - s_\theta(x_t) \big\|.$$
  • Large $\|s_\theta^\Delta\|$ ⇒ strong text guidance ⇒ memorization
  • Cheap, model-only, runs at any sampling step
  • Heuristic: no theoretical justification provided
Wen, Liu, Chen, Lyu, "Detecting, Explaining, and Mitigating Memorization in Diffusion Models", ICLR 2024.

Geometric View — LID (Ross 2024)

Local Intrinsic Dimensionality of a generated sample.

Exact mem.

$\text{LID} \approx 0$ — pure copy.

Partial mem.

Small but nonzero LID.

Generalized

Moderate LID, sample variability.

Limit: LID is informative only at the final step $t \approx 0$.

Ross, Kamkari, Wu, Hosseinzadeh, Liu, Stein, Cresswell, Loaiza-Ganem, "A Geometric Framework for Understanding Memorization in Generative Models", arXiv 2411.00113, 2024.

Our Method: SAIL

Hessian eigenvalues, score-norm justification, noise optimization

Sharpness of $\log p_t$

Hessian of the log-density (Jacobian of the score):

$$H(x_t) \;:=\; \nabla_{x_t}^2 \log p_t(x_t),\qquad H(x_t, c) \;:=\; \nabla_{x_t}^2 \log p_t(x_t \mid c).$$
  • Eigenvectors: principal axes of curvature
  • Negative eigenvalues: local concavity (peaked density)
  • Large $|\lambda|$: steep change in $\log p_t$

Sharpness = magnitude of negative eigenvalues of $H(x_t)$.

Memorization $\Leftrightarrow$ Sharp Peaks

  • Memorized sample: large negative eigenvalues of $H_\theta(x_t, c)$ — isolated spike
  • Generalized sample: near-zero or positive eigenvalues — smooth basin

Sharpness is a per-sample, per-timestep signal.

Validated on 2D Gaussian mixture, MNIST, and Stable Diffusion v1.4 / v2.0 latent space.

Holds at $t = T-1$ — the very first sampling step.

Score Norm as Sharpness

Computing the full Hessian spectrum is intractable in latent space ($d = 16{,}384$).

Lemma 4.1 (Jeon, Kim, No 2025).

For Gaussian $x \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$ with $H(x) = -\Sigma^{-1}$:

$$\mathbb{E}\big[\,\| s(x) \|^2\,\big] \;=\; -\,\mathrm{tr}\big( H(x) \big).$$

Score norm $\|s_\theta(x_t)\|^2$ tracks $-\mathrm{tr}(H_\theta(x_t))$ — tractable proxy.

Wen's Metric Is a Sharpness Measure

Lemma 4.2 (Jeon, Kim, No 2025).

For $x \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$ and $x \mid c \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu_c, \Sigma_c)$ with commuting covariances and $\mu = \mu_c$:

$$\mathbb{E}\!\left[\,\| s(x, c) - s(x) \|^2\,\right] \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{d} \frac{(\lambda_i - \lambda_{i,c})^2}{\lambda_{i,c}},$$

$\lambda_i, \lambda_{i,c}$: eigenvalues of $H(x), H_c(x)$.

Wen's $\|s_\theta^\Delta\|$ measures the squared eigenvalue gap between conditional and unconditional Hessians.

Hessian-Score Product — Cubic Amplification

At $t = T-1$ the latent is near-isotropic Gaussian — Wen's gap shrinks.

Lemma 4.3 (Jeon, Kim, No 2025).

For Gaussian $x \sim \mathcal{N}(\mu, \Sigma)$:

$$\mathbb{E}\!\left[\,\| H(x)\,s(x) \|^2\,\right] \;=\; -\,\mathrm{tr}\!\big( H(x)^3 \big).$$

Multiply score by Hessian ⇒ cube the eigenvalues — high-curvature directions amplified.

Proposed Detection Metric

Hessian-score product on the conditional gap, with $H^\Delta = H_\theta(x_t,c) - H_\theta(x_t)$ and $s^\Delta = s_\theta(x_t,c) - s_\theta(x_t)$:

$$\mathcal{M}(x_t, c) \;:=\; \big\| H^\Delta\,s^\Delta \big\|^2,\qquad \mathbb{E}[\mathcal{M}] \;=\; \sum_{i=1}^{d} \frac{(\lambda_i - \lambda_{i,c})^4}{\lambda_{i,c}}.$$

Quartic eigenvalue gap — sharper signal at the first step.

Detection Results — Stable Diffusion

500 memorized prompts (Webster) vs 500 non-memorized (COCO / Lexica / Tuxemon / GPT-4).

SD v1.4, $n = 4$, step 1

Wen: AUC 0.832 / TPR@1% 0.124

Ours: AUC 0.998 / TPR@1% 0.982

SD v2.0, $n = 4$, step 1

Wen: AUC 0.851 / TPR@1% 0.000

Ours: AUC 0.991 / TPR@1% 0.949

One Hessian-vector product, one step — matches Wen's full 50-step performance.

SAIL — Mitigation by Initialization

DDIM is a deterministic noise-to-image map.

Sharp $x_T$ ⇒ sharp trajectory ⇒ memorized image.

Search instead for $x_T$ in a smooth region of $p_T(\cdot \mid c)$.

$$\min_{x_T}\;\; \big\| H_\theta^\Delta(x_T)\,s_\theta^\Delta(x_T) \big\|^2 \;-\; \alpha \log p_G(x_T),$$

$p_G$: isotropic Gaussian prior, $\alpha$: prior weight.

No retraining, no prompt edit — only the seed moves.

Taylor Approximation

Backprop through a Hessian-vector product is expensive. Replace with finite difference:

$$\big\| H_\theta^\Delta(x_T)\,s_\theta^\Delta(x_T) \big\|^2 \;\approx\; \frac{\big\| s_\theta^\Delta\!\big(x_T + \delta\,s_\theta^\Delta(x_T)\big) \;-\; s_\theta^\Delta(x_T) \big\|^2}{\delta^2}.$$

One additional forward pass, no second-order autograd.

$\delta$ small: Hessian-vector product; $\delta$ large: still curvature-sensitive.

Final SAIL Objective

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{SAIL}}(x_T) \;=\; \big\| s_\theta^\Delta\!\big(x_T + \delta\,s_\theta^\Delta(x_T)\big) - s_\theta^\Delta(x_T) \big\|^2 \;+\; \alpha\,\| x_T \|^2.$$
  • Initialize $x_T \sim \mathcal{N}(0, I)$
  • Adam steps on $\mathcal{L}_{\text{SAIL}}$ at lr $0.05$
  • Early stop on threshold $\ell_{\text{thres}}$ to keep $x_T$ Gaussian-typical
  • Run normal DDIM with the optimized seed
Jeon, Kim, No, "Understanding and Mitigating Memorization in Generative Models via Sharpness of Probability Landscapes", ICML 2025.

Mitigation Results

Lower SSCD ⇒ less copy. Higher CLIP ⇒ better prompt fidelity.

RTA / RNA / Wen / Ren

Modify text embedding or attention.

Reduce copy — also degrade prompt alignment.

SAIL (ours)

Modify only the seed $x_T$.

Best Pareto front on SSCD vs CLIP for SD v1.4 and v2.0.

Prompt and weights untouched — user intent fully preserved.

CLIP Embeddings Drive Memorization

Padding tokens, not prompt tokens, do the work

SD Text Conditioning — Four Slots

CLIP text encoder pads every prompt to 77 tokens. Embedding sequence has four roles:

Start-of-text $\langle\text{SoT}\rangle$

Position 0, fixed.

Prompt tokens

Carry user content.

End-of-text $\langle\text{EoT}\rangle$

Only embedding optimized in CLIP contrastive loss.

Padding

Default pad token = $\langle\text{EoT}\rangle$ token.

Padding Embedding $\approx$ EoT Embedding

SD's tokenizer pads with the EoT id. CLIP is causal:

$$e_t \;=\; \mathrm{Transformer}\big( e_{\le t}\big),\qquad e_{\text{pad}_k} \;\approx\; e_{\text{EoT}} \quad \text{for many } k.$$
  • Padding slots inherit the EoT representation almost identically
  • One trained vector ($e_{\text{EoT}}$) is replicated across $\sim 60$ slots
  • Cross-attention over-weights this vector during generation

For memorized prompts: prompt-token embeddings contribute negligibly.

Mitigation I — Replace Pad + Mask EoT

  • Replace tokenizer's default pad id from $\langle\text{EoT}\rangle$ to a neutral token (e.g. !)
  • Mask the EoT embedding in cross-attention (zero its key/value)

Padding slots no longer carry the EoT vector; cross-attention loses its memorization anchor.

No retraining, no prompt edit, no detection step.

Mitigation II — Partial Padding Mask

Lighter intervention: keep tokenizer; mask only padding embeddings beyond a cutoff.

  • Reduces SSCD copy on Webster's memorized prompt set
  • Preserves CLIP prompt alignment
  • Drop-in replacement at inference time
Kim and No, "Memorization in Stable Diffusion Is Unexpectedly Driven by CLIP Embeddings", CVPR 2026 Findings.

Defining LLM Memorization

From canaries to extractable strings

Generalization vs Memorization

Deep nets fit pure random labels — capacity to memorize is not in dispute.

  • Question is what is retained after training
  • Generalization can coexist with memorization of rare points

Memorization is a per-sample property, not an aggregate.

Zhang, Bengio, Hardt, Recht, Vinyals, "Understanding Deep Learning Requires Rethinking Generalization", ICLR 2017.

Secret Sharer (Carlini 2019)

Inject a synthetic secret into training:

$$s \;=\; \text{``My SSN is 281-26-5017''},\qquad s \sim \mathrm{Unif}(\mathcal{R}).$$
  • Template randomness space $\mathcal{R}$ controls canary entropy $H(s) = \log_2 |\mathcal{R}|$
  • Single insertion into a real training corpus
  • Quantify unintended memorization after training
Carlini, Liu, Erlingsson, Kos, Song, "The Secret Sharer: Evaluating and Testing Unintended Memorization in Neural Networks", USENIX Security 2019.

Exposure — Calibrated Memorization Score

Rank-based score against the canary template's randomness space:

$$\mathrm{exposure}_\theta(s) \;=\; \log_2 |\mathcal{R}| \;-\; \log_2 \mathrm{rank}_\theta(s).$$
  • 0 bits: rank uniform — canary unmemorized
  • $\log_2 |\mathcal{R}|$ bits: rank 1 — canary is the top completion

Calibrated against the prior — not raw perplexity.

Null Distribution — Tail Bound

Under $H_0$ (no memorization), $\mathrm{rank}_\theta(s) \sim \mathrm{Unif}\{1, \ldots, |\mathcal{R}|\}$:

$$\Pr\nolimits_{H_0}\!\big[\,\mathrm{exposure}(s) \ge t\,\big] \;\le\; 2^{-t}.$$

Geometric tail ⇒ calibrated false-positive rate.

Exposure is in bits, comparable across canaries and models.

Long-Tail Theorem (Feldman)

Counterfactual memorization: with-vs-without difference.

$$\mathrm{mem}(A, \mathcal{D}, z) \;=\; \Pr_A\big[A(\mathcal{D}) \text{ predicts } z\big] - \Pr_A\big[A(\mathcal{D}\setminus z) \text{ predicts } z\big].$$

Theorem (Feldman 2020).

On distributions with singleton mass $\pi_1$, any $\varepsilon$-near-optimal learner must satisfy

$$\mathbb{E}_{z \sim \mathcal{D}}\!\big[\mathrm{mem}(A, \mathcal{D}, z)\big] \;=\; \Omega(\pi_1 - \varepsilon).$$
Feldman, "Does Learning Require Memorization? A Short Tale About a Long Tail", STOC 2020.

Extraction Attacks

From GPT-2 to scaling laws to Min-K

Extracting GPT-2 (Carlini 2021)

Definition (Carlini 2021).

String $s$ is $k$-extractable from $f_\theta$ if there exists a prefix $p$ with $|p| \le k$ such that

$$f_\theta(\mathrm{greedy}, p) \;=\; s.$$

Black-box attack on GPT-2: recovered names, emails, phones, code, UUIDs — some present once in training.

Carlini, Tramer, Wallace, Jagielski, Herbert-Voss, Lee, Roberts, Brown, Song, Erlingsson, Oprea, Raffel, "Extracting Training Data from Large Language Models", USENIX Security 2021.

The Extraction Pipeline

  1. 1. Generate $N$ samples with diverse prompts
  2. 2. Rank by signal $\Lambda(x) = \log p_\theta(x) / \log p_{\text{ref}}(x)$
  3. 3. Verify top candidates against the corpus or web

Same pipeline reused by every later extraction paper.

Three Scaling Laws (Carlini 2022)

Memorization grows log-linearly along three axes.

  • Model size — larger models memorize more
  • Data repetition — duplicates dominate
  • Context length — longer prefix, easier extraction

Even singletons are memorized at sufficient scale.

Carlini, Ippolito, Jagielski, Lee, Tramèr, Zhang, "Quantifying Memorization Across Neural Language Models", ICLR 2023.

Repetition — Empirical Law

For $k$ duplicates of $s$ in training:

$$\Pr\!\big[\,s \text{ is extractable}\,\big] \;\approx\; 1 - \big(1 - p_0(s, \theta)\big)^k.$$

Per-occurrence rate $p_0$ is log-linear in the three axes:

$$\log p_0(s, \theta) \;\propto\; \log N_\theta \;+\; \log L_{\mathrm{ctx}} \;-\; \log\tfrac{1}{\mathrm{rarity}(s)}.$$

Additive after log — risks compose independently.

Deduplication Helps (Lee 2022)

Near-duplicate removal in C4 / Wiki40B.

  • Memorized emissions drop by 10$\times$
  • Validation perplexity unchanged or improved
  • Cheap mitigation, orthogonal to differential privacy

Now standard in production data pipelines.

Lee, Ippolito, Nystrom, Zhang, Eck, Callison-Burch, Carlini, "Deduplicating Training Data Makes Language Models Better", ACL 2022.

Aligned Models Still Leak (Nasr 2023)

Divergence attack on aligned ChatGPT.

  • Prompt: "Repeat the word 'poem' forever"
  • Model breaks chat persona, emits training fragments
  • Hundreds of MB recovered for $200 of API spend

RLHF alignment is a filter, not memory removal.

Nasr, Carlini, Hayase, Jagielski, Cooper, Ippolito, Choquette-Choo, Wallace, Tramèr, Lee, "Scalable Extraction of Training Data from (Production) Language Models", arXiv 2311.17035, 2023.

Min-K% Prob (Shi 2024)

Reference-free pretraining-data MIA.

For tokens $x_t$ in candidate $x$, score by mean log-prob over the lowest-$K\%$ positions:

$$\mathrm{MinK}\%(x) \;=\; \frac{1}{|S|}\!\sum_{x_t \in S} \log p_\theta(x_t \mid x_{<t}),\qquad S = \text{bottom-}K\%\,.$$
  • Members keep their unlikely tokens above the threshold
  • Non-members have hard tokens that drag the mean down
  • No reference model, no training-shadow models
Shi, Ajith, Xia, Huang, Liu, Blevins, Chen, Zettlemoyer, "Detecting Pretraining Data from Large Language Models", ICLR 2024.

Min-K%++ Probe (Zhang 2025)

Calibrate per-position via a $z$-score under the next-token distribution:

$$\mathrm{MinK}\%\text{++}(x) \;=\; \frac{1}{|S|}\sum_{x_t \in S} \frac{\log p_\theta(x_t \mid x_{<t}) - \mu_t}{\sigma_t},$$

$\mu_t, \sigma_t$: mean and std of $\log p_\theta(\cdot \mid x_{<t})$ over the vocabulary.

  • Robust to position-wise scale of log-likelihood
  • SOTA on WikiMIA / MIMIR pretraining-data MIA
  • Bridges memorization-detection and MIA literatures
Zhang, Liu, Lyu, He, Lin, "Min-K%++: Improved Baseline for Detecting Pre-Training Data", ICLR 2025.

Adversarial Compression and Beyond

ACR, books, defenses

Why a New Definition?

Existing definitions miss the compliance audit use case.

  • Discoverable (Carlini 2023): too permissive — defeated by tiny refusal trigger
  • Extractable (Nasr 2023): too restrictive — "repeat after me" wins trivially
  • Counterfactual (Zhang 2023): retraining cost — impractical legally
  • MIA / perplexity: brittle to small weight edits — spoofable

Need: adversarial, completion-free, auditable on a single string.

Adversarial Compression Ratio (ACR)

Definition (Schwarzschild 2024).

For target token sequence $y$ and model $M$,

$$\mathrm{ACR}(M, y) \;=\; \frac{|y|}{|x^*|},\qquad x^* \;=\; \arg\min_x |x| \;\;\text{s.t.}\;\; M(x) = y.$$

$y$ is $\tau$-memorized if $\mathrm{ACR}(M, y) > \tau(y)$. Default $\tau = 1$ (any compression).

String memorized $\Leftrightarrow$ a shorter prompt elicits it — weights must store it.

Schwarzschild, Feng, Maini, Lipton, Kolter, "Rethinking LLM Memorization through the Lens of Adversarial Compression", NeurIPS 2024.

MiniPrompt — Search by GCG

Outer loop over prompt length, inner loop is GCG (Zou et al. 2023).

  1. 1. Initialize $z^{(0)}$ as 5 random tokens
  2. 2. Run $n$ GCG steps on $z^{(i)}$ to maximize $p_M(y \mid z^{(i)})$
  3. 3. If $M(z^{(i)}) = y$: shrink length by 1; else grow by 5
  4. 4. Return shortest successful $|x^*|$

Discrete search — arbitrary tokens, no semantic constraint.

ACR — What It Reveals

  • Famous quotes: high ACR — flagged as memorized
  • Post-training-cutoff text: low ACR — not memorized
  • Models told to "forget" copyrighted snippets retain almost the same ACR

"Unlearning" via finetuning is often an illusion of compliance — ACR sees through it.

ACR is completion-free: no prefix split, no pre-set length parameters.

Probabilistic Extraction (Hayes 2024)

Greedy verbatim is too narrow — sampled completions also leak.

$$M_n(s, p) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{\hat s \sim f_\theta(\cdot \mid p)}\!\Big[\,\mathbb{1}\big[\mathrm{LCS}(s, \hat s) \ge n\big]\,\Big].$$
  • $n$-gram overlap probability under the model's own sampler
  • Greedy = corner case ($n = |s|$)
  • Captures soft / partial memorization at realistic decoding
Hayes, Cooper, Lee, Choquette-Choo, Carlini, "Measuring Memorization via Probabilistic Extraction", arXiv 2410.19482, 2024.

Books from Open-Weight LLMs (Cooper 2025)

50 books $\times$ 17 open-weight models, probabilistic extraction.

  • Most LLMs do not memorize most books
  • LLAMA 3.1 70B memorizes Harry Potter 1 and 1984 nearly verbatim
  • Memorization is book-specific and model-specific

Polarized lawsuit positions oversimplify reality.

Cooper, Gokaslan, Ahmed, Cyphert, De Sa, Lemley, Ho, Liang, "Extracting Memorized Pieces of (Copyrighted) Books from Open-Weight LLMs", arXiv 2505.12546, 2025.

Non-Adversarial Reproduction (Aerni 2024)

Even benign user prompts produce overlap with training data.

  • $\sim 1\%$ of natural-prompt outputs share > 50-token spans with the web
  • Higher in code-completion and Q&A traffic
  • No adversarial intent required

Memorization leaks in normal use, not just under attack.

Aerni, Rando, Debenedetti, Carlini, Ippolito, Tramèr, "Measuring Non-Adversarial Reproduction of Training Data in Large Language Models", arXiv 2411.10242, 2024.

Defenses

Deduplication

Cheap, orthogonal, partial.

$10\times$ drop in emissions.

DP training

Provable per-sample bound.

Utility cost; rare in production LLMs.

Unlearning

Targeted removal of $\mathcal{D}_f$.

Open: deletion vs suppression.

None is a silver bullet. Stack them — and audit with ACR.

Memorization vs Membership Inference

MIA

Was $x \in \mathcal{D}$?

Distinguishability between two posteriors.

One bit of leakage per query.

Memorization

Can $x$ be reproduced verbatim?

Constructive recovery of content.

String-level leakage.

MIA is necessary; memorization is the stronger signal.

Open Problems

  • Concept memorization — style, character, code idiom
  • Per-sample auditing on $10^{12}$-token corpora
  • Multi-modal extraction — image, audio, video
  • Memorization in agents and RAG — caches and retrieval stores
  • Provable extraction lower bounds for closed models
  • Sharpness-style metrics for autoregressive token distributions

Takeaways

  • Diffusion: memorization = sharp peaks of $\log p_t$ — SAIL fixes the seed
  • SD: padding embeddings are the actual memorization channel
  • LLM: exposure, $k$-extractable, three scaling laws all work
  • ACR is the audit-grade definition — resistant to compliance theatre
  • Per-sample, per-prompt, per-string — never aggregate
