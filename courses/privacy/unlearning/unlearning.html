01

Motivation and Definition

From the right to be forgotten to certified deletion

The Central Question

Trained model $\theta_o = A(\mathcal{D})$, forget set $\mathcal{D}_f \subset \mathcal{D}$:

Produce $\theta_u$ that behaves as if $\mathcal{D}_f$ were never used.

Cheaper than retraining $A(\mathcal{D} \setminus \mathcal{D}_f)$ from scratch.

Should match retrain in distribution, not just outputs.

Why Now

Lawsuits

NYT v. OpenAI · Disney v. Midjourney · Getty v. Stability AI.

GDPR Art. 17

Right to erasure within ~one month of request.

Memorization

Verbatim recovery of books, PII (Carlini, Cooper).

Unlearning is the technical answer to a regulatory and legal question.

The Retraining Baseline

Gold standard: retrain $A(\mathcal{D}_r)$ on $\mathcal{D}_r = \mathcal{D} \setminus \mathcal{D}_f$.

  • Provides exact unlearning by construction
  • Cost: full training pass — prohibitive for foundation models
  • Every method below approximates this baseline

Quality of approximation = the central evaluation question.

Data-Deletion Operation (Ginart 2019)

Definition.

$R_A(\mathcal{D}, A(\mathcal{D}), i)$ is a deletion operation for algorithm $A$ if for every $\mathcal{D}, i$:

$$A(\mathcal{D}_{-i}) \;=_d\; R_A(\mathcal{D}, A(\mathcal{D}), i).$$

Equality is in distribution, not on a single sample path.

Ginart, Guan, Valiant, Zou, "Making AI Forget You: Data Deletion in Machine Learning", NeurIPS 2019.

Exact vs Approximate

Exact

Distribution match $A(\mathcal{D}_r) =_d \mathcal{U}(\mathcal{D}, \mathcal{D}_f, \theta_o)$.

Achieved by retrain or SISA-style sharding.

Approximate

$(\varepsilon, \delta)$-indistinguishability — borrow from DP.

Practical for deep nets; needs careful auditing.

Certified Unlearning

Definition (Zhang et al. 2024, after Dwork).

$\mathcal{U}$ is $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-certified if for all measurable $T$:

$$\Pr[\mathcal{U}(\mathcal{D}, \mathcal{D}_f, A(\mathcal{D})) \in T] \;\le\; e^\varepsilon \Pr[A(\mathcal{D}_r) \in T] + \delta,$$

and the symmetric direction holds.

Same machinery as DP — bounds the gap between unlearned and retrained.

Zhang, Dong, Wang, Li, "Towards Certified Unlearning for Deep Neural Networks", ICML 2024.

Theorem — Newton-Step Unlearning

Theorem (Guo et al. 2020).

Let $L$ be $\lambda$-strongly convex and $G$-Lipschitz on bounded $\theta$. The single Newton step

$$\theta_u \;=\; \theta_o \;+\; \tfrac{1}{n}\,H_{\theta_o}^{-1}\,\nabla_\theta \ell(\theta_o; z),$$

followed by Gaussian noise $\mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I)$ with

$$\sigma \;\ge\; \frac{2 G^2}{\lambda^2 n^2 \varepsilon}\sqrt{2\log(1.25/\delta)},$$

is an $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-certified deletion of $z$.

Guo, Goldstein, Hannun, van der Maaten, "Certified Data Removal from Machine Learning Models", ICML 2020.

Theorem — Deletion Capacity (Sekhari)

Theorem (Sekhari et al. 2021).

For convex $G$-Lipschitz, $L$-smooth losses in $d$ dimensions with $n$ training points and $m$ deletion requests, an $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-unlearner achieves

$$\mathbb{E}\!\left[L(\theta_u) - L(\theta^*)\right] \;\le\; \tilde O\!\left(\sqrt{\tfrac{d}{n}} \;+\; \tfrac{m\sqrt{d}}{n\varepsilon}\right),$$

provided $m \;\le\; n\varepsilon / \sqrt{d}$.

"Deletion capacity" = max $m$ before excess-risk crosses retrain.

Sekhari, Acharya, Kamath, Suresh, "Remember What You Want to Forget: Algorithms for Machine Unlearning", NeurIPS 2021.

SISA (Bourtoule et al.)

Sharded, Isolated, Sliced, Aggregated.

  • Partition $\mathcal{D}$ into $S$ shards, each into $R$ slices
  • Train one constituent model per shard, checkpoint at each slice
  • Aggregate predictions at inference

Unlearning $x$ ⇒ retrain only the affected shard from its last clean slice.

Exact, but limited: aggregation hurts utility, not for foundation models.

Bourtoule et al., "Machine Unlearning", IEEE S&P 2021.

Influence Function — Closed Form

First-order parameter shift on removing $z$:

$$\theta_{-z} - \theta_o \;\approx\; -\,\frac{1}{n}\,H_{\theta_o}^{-1}\,\nabla_\theta \ell(\theta_o; z), \qquad H_{\theta_o} = \nabla^2 L(\theta_o).$$

Used directly as IU (Influence Unlearning) and as the Newton update above.

  • Exact for quadratic loss; first-order Taylor for general convex
  • Hessian-vector products replace $H^{-1}$ in deep nets
  • Foundation underlying certified unlearning
Koh and Liang, "Understanding Black-box Predictions via Influence Functions", ICML 2017.

SCRUB (Kurmanji et al.)

Teacher = original $\theta_o$. Student = unlearned $\theta_u$.

$$\min_{\theta_u}\; \frac{1}{N_r}\!\!\sum_{x_r \in \mathcal{D}_r}\!\! d(x_r; \theta_u) \;-\; \frac{1}{N_f}\!\!\sum_{x_f \in \mathcal{D}_f}\!\! d(x_f; \theta_u),$$

$d(x; \theta_u) = D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p(\cdot;\theta_o) \,\|\, p(\cdot; \theta_u))$.

  • Stay close to teacher on retain, move away on forget
  • "Contrastive distillation"
Kurmanji, Triantafillou, Hayes, Triantafillou, "Towards Unbounded Machine Unlearning", NeurIPS 2023.

SalUn — Weight Saliency (Fan et al.)

Update only the weights that matter for the forget set:

$$\mathbf{m}_S \;=\; \mathbb{1}\!\left(\,\big|\nabla_\theta \ell_f(\theta;\mathcal{D}_f)\big|_{\theta = \theta_o} \;\ge\; \gamma \right),$$
$$\theta_u \;=\; \mathbf{m}_S \odot (\Delta\theta + \theta_o) \;+\; (\mathbf{1} - \mathbf{m}_S) \odot \theta_o.$$
  • $\gamma$ = median of saliency magnitudes
  • Combined with random-label loss for image classification
Fan, Liu, Zhang, Wong, Wei, Liu, "SalUn: Saliency-Based Unlearning", ICLR 2024 (spotlight).

$\ell_1$-Sparse Unlearning (Jia et al.)

Add sparsity penalty during the unlearning fine-tune:

$$\theta_u \;=\; \arg\min_\theta \; L_u(\theta;\theta_o,\mathcal{D}_r) \;+\; \gamma \|\theta\|_1.$$
  • Pruning emerges from the unlearning step itself
  • Sparser models forget more cleanly
  • Compatible with FT, GA, IU as the inner $L_u$
Jia, Liu, Ram, Yao, Liu, Liu, Sharma, Liu, "Model Sparsity Can Simplify Machine Unlearning", NeurIPS 2023 (spotlight).

Residual Knowledge Under Perturbation

Unlearned model can still classify perturbed forget samples correctly.

$$L_{\text{RURK}}(\mathbf{w}, \mathcal{S}) \;=\; \frac{1}{|\mathcal{S}_r|}\!\sum \ell(\mathbf{w}; (x,y))\;-\; \frac{\lambda}{|\mathcal{S}_f|}\!\sum \kappa(\mathbf{w}; (x,y)),$$

$\kappa$ averages loss over a neighborhood $\mathcal{V}((x,y),\tau)$.

Statistical indistinguishability is not robust to local perturbation.

Hsu, Niroula, He, Brugere, Lecue, Chen, "The Unseen Threat: Residual Knowledge in Machine Unlearning under Perturbed Samples", NeurIPS 2025.

Classification — Metrics

Full-stack evaluation and information-theoretic indices

Full-Stack MU Evaluation

  • UA — unlearning accuracy: $1 - \text{Acc}_{\mathcal{D}_f}(\theta_u)$ (vs Retrain, not max)
  • RA — accuracy on $\mathcal{D}_r$ (fidelity)
  • TA — test accuracy (generalization)
  • MIA-Efficacy — confidence MIA on $\mathcal{D}_f$
  • RTE — runtime cost vs full retrain

Single metrics mislead. The reference is Retrain, not the original model.

Jia et al., "Model Sparsity Can Simplify Machine Unlearning", NeurIPS 2023.

MIA as an Unlearning Probe

Yeom-style threshold attack on the forget set:

$$s_{\text{loss}}(x, y) \;=\; -\log p_\theta(y \mid x), \qquad \hat m \;=\; \mathbb{1}[\,s_{\text{loss}} \le \tau\,].$$

Entropy variant: $s_{\text{ent}}(x) = -\sum_c p_\theta(c \mid x) \log p_\theta(c \mid x)$.

  • Low loss / low entropy ⇒ likely member
  • Higher MIA-Efficacy ⇒ less leakage about $\mathcal{D}_f$

Information Difference Index (Lab)

Output-only metrics miss intermediate representations.

$$\mathrm{IDI}(\theta_u) \;=\; \frac{\sum_{\ell=1}^{L} \big(I(\mathbf{Z}_\ell^{(u)}; Y) - I(\mathbf{Z}_\ell^{(r)}; Y)\big)} {\sum_{\ell=1}^{L} \big(I(\mathbf{Z}_\ell^{(o)}; Y) - I(\mathbf{Z}_\ell^{(r)}; Y)\big)}.$$
  • $\mathrm{IDI} = 1$ ⇒ unlearned model = original (no forgetting)
  • $\mathrm{IDI} = 0$ ⇒ unlearned model = retrain (perfect forgetting)
Jeon, Jeung, Kim, No, Choi, "An Information-Theoretic Evaluation Metric for Strong Unlearning", AAAI 2026.

COLA — Collapse + Align (Lab)

Two-stage method matched to the IDI metric:

  • Collapse — drive forget representations to a uniform feature
  • Align — restore retain representations via head distillation

IDI as both evaluation and training signal.

Beats SCRUB / SalUn / $\ell_1$-sparse on intermediate-layer recovery.

Over-Unlearning: Negative IDI

SCRUB and some FT variants over-shoot the retrain reference.

  • $\mathrm{IDI} < 0$ at the final block
  • Unlearned representation has less $\mathcal{D}_f$ info than retrain
  • Manifests as MIA-Efficacy > Retrain — a red flag, not a win

Higher metric values are not always better — the target is match, not maximize.

04

LLM Unlearning

Why language models break the classification toolkit

Why LLMs Are Different

  • No clean class boundary — knowledge is distributed
  • Forget set is text, not labeled images — unit of forgetting unclear
  • Catastrophic forgetting under GA wipes general capability
  • Retraining baseline is unavailable at trillion-token scale

Most "LLM unlearning" today is output suppression, not deletion.

Gradient Ascent (Jang et al.)

Maximize NLL on the forget corpus:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\text{GA}}(\theta) \;=\; -\,\mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{D}_F}\!\big[\,-\log p_\theta(x)\,\big].$$
  • Equivalent to unlikelihood training (Welleck 2020)
  • Effective on knowledge probes — but degrades fluency fast
  • Often paired with descent on $\mathcal{D}_r$ to preserve utility
Jang, Yoon, Yang, Kim, Yang, Seo, "Knowledge Unlearning for Mitigating Privacy Risks in Language Models", ACL 2023.

Why GA Collapses

Gradient of GA loss explodes as the model unlearns:

$$\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{GA}}(\theta) \;=\; -\,\mathbb{E}_{x \sim \mathcal{D}_F}\!\!\left[\frac{\nabla_\theta p_\theta(x)}{p_\theta(x)}\right], \qquad \big\|\nabla_\theta \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{GA}}\big\| \to \infty \;\;\text{as}\;\; p_\theta(x) \to 0.$$

Effective step size grows during training ⇒ $\theta$ escapes any data distribution.

NPO replaces $-\log p_\theta$ with a sigmoid-saturated surrogate. That bounded-loss reformulation is the entire fix.

NPO — Negative Preference Optimization

DPO-style loss with only the negative branch:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{NPO}}(\theta) \;=\; \frac{2}{\beta}\,\mathbb{E}_{\mathcal{D}_{\mathrm{FG}}}\!\!\left[\log\!\left(1 + \Big(\tfrac{\pi_\theta(y\mid x)}{\pi_{\text{ref}}(y\mid x)}\Big)^{\!\beta}\right)\right].$$
  • Bounded gradient — avoids GA's catastrophic collapse
  • Reduces likelihood of forget responses, doesn't hard-zero them
  • Drop-in replacement for GA in most pipelines
Zhang, Lin, Bai, Mei, "Negative Preference Optimization: From Catastrophic Collapse to Effective Unlearning", COLM 2024.

NPO — Bounded Loss

With log-ratio $u = \beta \log(\pi_\theta / \pi_{\text{ref}})$:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{NPO}}(\theta) \;=\; \tfrac{2}{\beta}\,\mathbb{E}\!\left[\log(1 + e^{u})\right], \qquad \frac{\partial \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{NPO}}}{\partial u} \;=\; \frac{2}{\beta}\,\sigma(u) \;\le\; \tfrac{2}{\beta}.$$

Sigmoid saturation ⇒ gradient bounded uniformly in $\pi_\theta$.

Theorem (Zhang et al. 2024).

For $\beta > 0$, the NPO update has bounded per-step divergence rate from $\pi_{\text{ref}}$, in contrast to GA whose rate is unbounded.

SimNPO — Reference-Free (Fan 2024)

Drop $\pi_{\text{ref}}$; replace with a constant margin and length normalization:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{SimNPO}}(\theta) \;=\; \mathbb{E}\!\left[\log\!\Big(1 + \exp\!\big(\tfrac{\beta}{|y|}\log\pi_\theta(y \mid x) + \gamma\big)\Big)\right].$$
  • No reference model ⇒ saves memory + compute
  • Length-normalized; avoids long-sequence bias
  • Outperforms NPO on TOFU, MUSE benchmarks
Fan, Zhang, Yao, Liu, Wei, Liu, "SimNPO: Simple Negative Preference Optimization for Unlearning", NeurIPS 2024.

ME + GD (Yuan et al.)

Push forget tokens toward uniform distribution via KL:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ME}}(\mathcal{D}_F;\theta) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{(x,y)\sim \mathcal{D}_F}\!\!\left[\,\frac{1}{T}\sum_{t=1}^{T} D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\big(P_t \,\|\, \mathcal{U}_{[K]}\big)\,\right].$$

Combine with gradient descent on retain corpus:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ME+GD}}(\theta) \;=\; \alpha\,\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ME}}(\mathcal{D}_F;\theta) \;+\; \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{GD}}(\mathcal{D}_R;\theta).$$
Yuan, Pang, Du, Chen, Zhang, Lin, "A Closer Look at Machine Unlearning for Large Language Models", ICLR 2025.

Who's Harry Potter? (Eldan & Russinovich)

Three-step recipe to unlearn a specific corpus:

  • Identify "Harry Potter–specific" tokens via reinforced model contrast
  • Replace with generic alternatives in training data
  • Fine-tune Llama-7B on the swapped data

Result: model knows fantasy tropes, but not Harry / Hermione / Ron.

First demonstrated concept-level unlearning at LLM scale.

Eldan, Russinovich, "Who's Harry Potter? Approximate Unlearning in LLMs", 2023.

IDK Refusal (TOFU)

Replace forget answers with "I don't know" variants:

$$L_{\text{idk}} \;=\; L(S_R, w) \;+\; L(S_F^{\text{idk}}, w),$$

$x_{\text{idk}} = [q, a_{\text{idk}}]$ uses 1 of 100 refusal templates.

  • Stable; preserves utility on $S_R$ and world knowledge
  • Behavioral, not parametric — pure suppression
  • De-facto baseline on TOFU benchmark
Maini, Feng, Schwarzschild, Lipton, Kolter, "TOFU: A Task of Fictitious Unlearning for LLMs", COLM 2024.

Guardrail Baselines & ECO

Guardrail (Thaker)

System-prompt-only suppression matches expensive methods on TOFU.

Calls into question the need to touch weights at all.

ECO (Liu)

Classifier flags forget prompts; corrupts their input embedding.

Weights frozen — inference-time intervention.

Cheap baselines often beat sophisticated training-time methods.

ELM — Erasing Conceptual Knowledge

Build "expert" and "novice" prompts for a target concept.

  • Mix expert / novice distributions to define an erased target
  • Cross-entropy fine-tune toward the erased distribution
  • Effective on WMDP-style hazardous knowledge

Concept-level erasure without retrain.

Gandikota, Feucht, Marks, Bau, "Erasing Conceptual Knowledge from Language Models", NeurIPS 2025.

LUNAR — Activation Redirection

Linear intervention in residual stream:

$$\mathbf{r}_{\text{UV}}^{(\ell)} \;=\; \frac{1}{|D_{\text{ref}}|}\!\!\sum_{x \in D_{\text{ref}}}\!\! \mathbf{a}^{(\ell)}(x) \;-\; \frac{1}{|D_f|}\!\!\sum_{x \in D_f}\!\! \mathbf{a}^{(\ell)}(x).$$

Add the unlearning vector to forget-set activations only:

$$\mathbf{a}'^{(\ell)} = \mathbf{a}^{(\ell)} + \mathbb{1}[x \in D_f]\,\mathbf{r}_{\text{UV}}^{(\ell)}.$$

$D_{\text{ref}}$ can be safety prompts — does not require forget-set knowledge.

Shen, Qiu, Kurmanji, Iacob, Sani, Chen, Cancedda, Lane, "LLM Unlearning via Neural Activation Redirection", NeurIPS 2025.

Benchmarks and Failures

What we measure, where it breaks

TOFU

Synthetic biographies of fictitious authors fine-tuned into Llama / Phi.

  • Forget set: 1% / 5% / 10% of authors
  • Retain set: remaining authors
  • Real-author and world-fact probes for utility

Clean, controlled, but fictitious — not pre-training scale.

Maini et al., COLM 2024.

WMDP — Hazardous Knowledge

3,668 questions on bio-, chem-, cyber-weapons.

  • Goal: reduce malicious-use accuracy without harming MMLU
  • RMU baseline: hidden states uniform on forget data
  • Real-world stakes — closer to deployment
Li, Pan, Gopal et al., "The WMDP Benchmark", ICML 2024.

RWKU — Real-World Knowledge

Forget famous individuals (e.g. Stephen King) from a base LLM.

  • Forget probes: cloze, QA, adversarial prefix injection
  • Neighbor probes: related works that should remain
  • Utility: MMLU / BBH / TriviaQA / TruthfulQA / Alpaca-Eval

Tests neighborhood preservation, not just target suppression.

Jin, Cao, Wang, He et al., "RWKU: Benchmarking Real-World Knowledge Unlearning for LLMs", NeurIPS 2024 D&B.

MUSE — Six-Way Evaluation

  • No verbatim memorization
  • No knowledge memorization
  • No privacy leakage (MIA)
  • Utility preservation
  • Scalability
  • Sustainability under repeated requests

First benchmark to score data-owner and deployer expectations jointly.

Shi, Lee, Huang, Malladi et al., "MUSE: Machine Unlearning Six-Way Evaluation for LLMs", ICLR 2025.

Benign Relearning (Hu et al.)

Unlearn Harry Potter ⇒ relearn from GPT-4-generated summaries ⇒ memorization recovers.

  • Public, copyright-free data restores forgotten content
  • Suggests unlearning is obfuscation, not deletion
  • Relearning needs no overlap with original tokens
Hu, Fu, Wu, Smith, "Unlearning or Obfuscating? Jogging the Memory of Unlearned LLMs via Benign Relearning", ICLR 2025.

Syntax as the Hidden Driver (Lab)

What property of relearning data matters? Syntax, not topic.

  • Topically-relevant rewrites: forgotten content stays forgotten
  • Syntactically-similar rewrites with new entities: full recovery
  • Mitigation: syntactic-diversification of forget queries during unlearn
Yoon, Hong, Jeung, No, "Rethinking Benign Relearning: Syntax as the Hidden Driver of Unlearning Failures", ICLR 2026.

Five Mismatches (Cooper et al.)

  • Output suppression $\ne$ removal of training data
  • Removal of training data $\ne$ meaningful output suppression
  • Models $\ne$ their outputs
  • Models $\ne$ how outputs are used downstream
  • Unlearning has unintended consequences

"Machine unlearning" doesn't do what policymakers think it does.

Cooper, Choquette-Choo, Bogen, Klyman et al., "Machine Unlearning Doesn't Do What You Think", NeurIPS 2025.

Theorem — Black-Box Verification Hardness

Theorem (Pawelczyk et al. 2024).

For black-box LLMs without weight access, no auditor with $q$ queries can $(1-\alpha)$-distinguish $\theta_u$ from $\theta_o$ when

$$q \;\le\; \tilde\Omega\!\left(\tfrac{1}{\alpha^2}\,\mathrm{TV}(\theta_u, \theta_o)^{-2}\right).$$

Single-prompt audits are provably insufficient — verification needs distributional probes.

Implication: regulators relying on output-level checks under-detect failed unlearning.

Pawelczyk, Neel, Lakkaraju, "On the Privacy Risk of In-Context Learning and Unlearning Audits", 2024.

Are We Making Progress? (Triantafillou 2024)

NeurIPS 2023 unlearning competition — 1,000+ submissions across 4 GPUs.

  • Best entries beat naive FT by < 5% MIA gap
  • Score saturates near retrain on the held-out forget set
  • Cross-distribution generalization to other forget sets is poor

Performance on a fixed forget set $\ne$ a property of the unlearner.

Triantafillou, Pedregosa, Hayes, Kairouz et al., "Are We Making Progress in Unlearning?", ICLR 2024.

Position (Lab): the Term Is Overused

Proposal.

"Machine unlearning" should mean retraining indistinguishability on a precisely defined forget set.

Other goals deserve different names:

  • Output likelihood suppression
  • Internal-representation obfuscation
  • Knowledge editing
  • Behavioral refusal
  • Inference-time interventions
Yoon, Jun (anon. preview), "Position: The Term Machine Unlearning Is Overused in LLMs", ICML 2026 (under review).

DUSK — Do Not Unlearn Shared Knowledge (Lab)

$\mathcal{D}_f$ and $\mathcal{D}_r$ overlap in concepts. Naive unlearning destroys shared knowledge.

  • Forget unique facts in $\mathcal{D}_f$
  • Preserve shared knowledge across $\mathcal{D}_r^{(i)}$
  • Benchmark: Venn-style retain partitions
Jeung, Yoon, Hong, Kim, Han, Yu, No, "DUSK: Do Not Unlearn Shared Knowledge", 2025.

R-TOFU — Reasoning Models (Lab)

LRMs unlearn both the answer and the chain-of-thought.

  • Answer-only unlearning leaves CoT residue
  • CoT-only is the best gradient-ascent variant
  • Decoding-time tricks (ZeroThink, LessThink) reveal residual knowledge
Yoon, Jeung, No, "R-TOFU: Unlearning in Large Reasoning Models", EMNLP 2025 (oral).

SEPS — Separability Measure (Lab)

Mixed-prompt evaluation: forget + retain queries in one input.

  • RIS $\approx \underline{F}$ — untargeted unlearning collapses retain to forget baseline
  • $\underline{FF} > \underline{F} > R\underline{R}$ — targeted unlearning fails on multi-query

Single-query benchmarks systematically overestimate unlearning quality.

Jeung, Yoon, No, "SEPS: A Separability Measure for Robust Unlearning in LLMs", EMNLP 2025.

Random Thoughts

  • Unlearning vs data valuation, influence functions, NN invariance, MIA — adjacent problems
  • Impossibility: $X \sim P$ vs $Y \sim Q$ with one sample, neither distribution given
  • Corpus data is harder than image+label — no clean "remove sample $i$" semantics

The hardest open question: what is the unit of forgetting in an LLM?

Takeaways

  • Definition: distributional match to retrain — anything else is suppression
  • Classification: SCRUB / SalUn / $\ell_1$-sparse all approximate retrain on different axes
  • Evaluate against Retrain, not against original — match is the goal
  • LLM "unlearning" is mostly behavioral; benign relearning recovers content
  • Benchmarks (TOFU, WMDP, MUSE) help, but single-query suites mislead — see SEPS
