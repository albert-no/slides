01

Beyond KL

Likelihood alone fails; a broader family helps

Recall — ML as KL Minimization

Given i.i.d. samples from $p_{\mathrm{data}}$, maximum likelihood picks

$$\hat\theta \;=\; \arg\min_{\theta}\, D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(p_{\mathrm{data}}\,\Vert\,p_\theta\bigr) \;=\; \arg\min_{\theta}\,\mathbb{E}_{p_{\mathrm{data}}}\!\bigl[-\log p_\theta(X)\bigr].$$

Two questions follow.

  • Does high likelihood imply good samples?
  • Is KL the only sensible training distance?

Answer to both: no. The rest of this lecture explains why.

Pathology 1 — High Likelihood, Bad Samples

Let $p_\theta(x) \;=\; 0.01\,p_{\mathrm{data}}(x) + 0.99\,p_{\mathrm{noise}}(x)$. Then

$$\log p_\theta(x) \;\ge\; \log\bigl(0.01\,p_{\mathrm{data}}(x)\bigr) \;=\; \log p_{\mathrm{data}}(x) - \log 100.$$

Taking $\mathbb{E}_{p_{\mathrm{data}}}$ gives the two-sided bound

$$-H(p_{\mathrm{data}}) - \log 100 \;\le\; \mathbb{E}_{p_{\mathrm{data}}}\!\bigl[\log p_\theta(X)\bigr] \;\le\; -H(p_{\mathrm{data}}).$$

Sample from $p_\theta$ ⇒ $99\%$ noise. Likelihood off by only $\log 100$.

Theis, van den Oord, and Bethge, ICLR 2016.

Pathology 2 — Overfit Loses on Sampling

Uniform $p_{\mathrm{data}}$ on $N$ atoms; $n < N$ samples observed; model is uniform on those $n$:

$$\mathbb{E}_{p_{\mathrm{data}}}\!\bigl[\log p_\theta(X)\bigr] \;=\; \tfrac{n}{N}\log\tfrac{1}{n} \;+\; \tfrac{N-n}{N}\log 0 \;=\; -\infty.$$

Yet every sample of $p_\theta$ is a real data point.

ML punishes the model that memorizes; quality and likelihood point in opposite directions.

Plan — a Family of Divergences

KL is one member of a much larger family parameterized by a convex $f$.

$$D_f(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_q\!\left[\, f\!\Bigl(\tfrac{p(X)}{q(X)}\Bigr)\,\right].$$
  • Catalogue. KL, reverse KL, TV, $\chi^2$, hockey-stick, JS.
  • Properties. Non-negativity, monotonicity, DPI, all at once.
  • Application. GAN training minimizes the JS divergence.
02

$f$-Divergence

One definition; six familiar instances

Definition

Definition ($f$-divergence).

Let $f:(0,\infty)\to\mathbb{R}$ be convex with $f(1)=0$. For pmfs $p,q$ on $\mathcal{X}$,

$$D_f(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; \sum_{x\in\mathcal{X}} q(x)\, f\!\Bigl(\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\Bigr).$$

Conventions: $0\cdot f(0/0) = 0$,   $f(0) = \lim_{t\to 0^+} f(t)$,   $0\cdot f(a/0) = \lim_{t\to 0^+} t\,f(a/t)$ for $a>0$.

For pdfs replace the sum by an integral against $q(x)\,dx$.

Reading the Definition

  • Reference measure $q$: expectation taken under $q$.
  • Likelihood ratio $r(x)=p(x)/q(x)$: the random object $f$ acts on.
  • $f(1)=0$: if $p=q$ then $r\equiv 1$ and the divergence vanishes.
  • Convexity of $f$: gives $D_f(p\,\Vert\,q)\ge 0$ by Jensen.
  • Asymmetric in general: swap $p\leftrightarrow q$ via $\widetilde f(t)=t\,f(1/t)$.

Example 1 — Relative Entropy

Take $f(t) = t\log t$. Then $f(1)=0$, $f''(t)=1/t>0$, and

$$D_f(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; \sum_x q(x)\,\frac{p(x)}{q(x)}\log\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \;=\; \sum_x p(x)\log\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p\,\Vert\,q).$$

The familiar KL divergence is the prototypical $f$-divergence.

Example 2 — Reverse KL and Total Variation

Reverse KL. Take $f(t)=-\log t$:

$$D_f(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; -\sum_x q(x)\log\frac{p(x)}{q(x)} \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\,\Vert\,p).$$

Total variation. Take $f(t)=\tfrac{1}{2}|t-1|$:

$$D_f(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_x q(x)\Bigl|\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)} - 1\Bigr| \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_x |p(x)-q(x)| \;=\; \mathrm{TV}(p,q).$$

Example 3 — $\chi^2$ Divergence

Take $f(t)=(t-1)^2$, convex with $f(1)=0$. Then

$$\chi^2(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; \sum_x q(x)\Bigl(\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)} - 1\Bigr)^{\!2} \;=\; \sum_x \frac{(p(x)-q(x))^2}{q(x)}.$$

Quadratic in the gap $p-q$; dominates KL via Pinsker-type bounds.

$\chi^2$ is the second-order Taylor of $D_{\mathrm{KL}}$ around $p=q$.

Example 4 — Hockey-Stick Divergence

For $\gamma \ge 1$, take $f(t)=[t-\gamma]_+ = \max\{t-\gamma,\,0\}$:

$$E_\gamma(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; \sum_x q(x)\bigl[\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)} - \gamma\bigr]_+ \;=\; \sum_x \bigl[p(x) - \gamma\,q(x)\bigr]_+.$$

At $\gamma=1$ this reduces to TV. Key role: $(\varepsilon,\delta)$-DP characterized by $E_{e^\varepsilon}(p\,\Vert\,q) \le \delta$.

Barthe and Olmedo, $f$-divergence composition, ICALP 2013.

Example 5 — Jensen–Shannon Divergence

Define

$$D_{\mathrm{JS}}(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\Bigl(p\,\Bigl\Vert\,\tfrac{p+q}{2}\Bigr) \;+\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\Bigl(q\,\Bigl\Vert\,\tfrac{p+q}{2}\Bigr).$$

Symmetric in $(p,q)$; bounded above by $2\log 2$; $\sqrt{D_{\mathrm{JS}}}$ is a metric.

JS is the divergence GAN actually minimizes — see Section 04.

JS as an $f$-Divergence

Take $f(t) = t\log\tfrac{2t}{t+1} + \log\tfrac{2}{t+1}$. Substitute $r=p/q$ into $\sum_x q\,f(r)$:

$$\begin{aligned} \sum_x q(x)\,f\!\bigl(\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}\bigr) &= \sum_x p(x)\log\frac{2p(x)}{p(x)+q(x)} + \sum_x q(x)\log\frac{2q(x)}{p(x)+q(x)} \\ &= D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\Bigl(p\,\Bigl\Vert\,\tfrac{p+q}{2}\Bigr) + D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\Bigl(q\,\Bigl\Vert\,\tfrac{p+q}{2}\Bigr) \\ &= D_{\mathrm{JS}}(p\,\Vert\,q). \end{aligned}$$

$f$ is convex with $f(1)=0$; the JS divergence is therefore an $f$-divergence.

Worked Example — Two Bernoullis

$p = \mathrm{Bern}(0.9)$, $q = \mathrm{Bern}(0.5)$.

$$\begin{array}{l|c} \text{Divergence} & \text{Value (nats)} \\[2pt]\hline\\[-6pt] D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p\,\Vert\,q) = 0.9\log\tfrac{0.9}{0.5}+0.1\log\tfrac{0.1}{0.5} & 0.368 \\[3pt] D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\,\Vert\,p) = 0.5\log\tfrac{0.5}{0.9}+0.5\log\tfrac{0.5}{0.1} & 0.510 \\[3pt] \mathrm{TV}(p,q) = \tfrac12(|0.9-0.5|+|0.1-0.5|) & 0.400 \\[3pt] \chi^2(p\,\Vert\,q) = \tfrac{(0.9-0.5)^2}{0.5}+\tfrac{(0.1-0.5)^2}{0.5} & 0.640 \\[3pt] D_{\mathrm{JS}}(p\,\Vert\,q) & 0.187 \end{array}$$

Asymmetry of KL: 0.368 vs 0.510. JS sits between TV and $\chi^2$ in magnitude.

03

Properties

DPI, information inequality, joint vs marginal

Overview — Four Properties at Once

Convexity of $f$ delivers four results that each held for $D_{\mathrm{KL}}$:

  • Joint $=$ marginal under a shared conditional: $D_f(p(x)p(y|x)\,\Vert\,q(x)p(y|x)) = D_f(p\,\Vert\,q)$.
  • Monotonicity in extension: adding a $Y$ coordinate can only increase $D_f$.
  • Data processing inequality: pushing $p,q$ through a channel cannot increase $D_f$.
  • Information inequality: $D_f(p\,\Vert\,q)\ge 0$, equality iff $p=q$ when $f$ is strictly convex at $1$.

All four follow from Jensen on the convex $f$; proofs on the next four slides.

Theorem — Properties of $f$-Divergence

Theorem (Csiszár, 1967).

Let $f:(0,\infty)\to\mathbb{R}$ be convex with $f(1)=0$. For pmfs $p,q$ on $\mathcal{X}$ and channel $p(y|x)$ on $\mathcal{X}\to\mathcal{Y}$:

  1. $D_f\!\bigl(p(x)p(y|x)\,\Vert\,q(x)p(y|x)\bigr) \;=\; D_f(p\,\Vert\,q)$.
  2. $D_f\!\bigl(p(x,y)\,\Vert\,q(x,y)\bigr) \;\ge\; D_f(p\,\Vert\,q)$.
  3. $D_f\!\bigl(p(y)\,\Vert\,q(y)\bigr) \;\le\; D_f(p\,\Vert\,q)$  (DPI).
  4. $D_f(p\,\Vert\,q) \ge 0$; $=0$ iff $p=q$ when $f$ is strictly convex at $1$.
Csiszár, Information-type measures of difference, Studia Sci. Math. Hungar. 1967.

Proof — Part 1 (Joint via Product Conditional)

Let $r(x,y) = \tfrac{p(x)p(y|x)}{q(x)p(y|x)} = \tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}$. Expand the joint $f$-divergence:

$$\begin{aligned} D_f\!\bigl(p(x)p(y|x)\,\Vert\,q(x)p(y|x)\bigr) &= \sum_{x,y} q(x)p(y|x)\,f\!\bigl(\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}\bigr) \\ &= \sum_{x} q(x)\,f\!\bigl(\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}\bigr)\,\underbrace{\sum_y p(y|x)}_{=1} \\ &= D_f(p\,\Vert\,q). \end{aligned}$$

The conditional $p(y|x)$ cancels because it appears in numerator and denominator.

Proof — Part 2 (Joint $\ge$ Marginal)

For any joints $p(x,y), q(x,y)$ on $\mathcal{X}\times\mathcal{Y}$:

$$\begin{aligned} D_f\!\bigl(p(x,y)\,\Vert\,q(x,y)\bigr) &= \sum_{x,y} q(x,y)\,f\!\bigl(\tfrac{p(x,y)}{q(x,y)}\bigr) \\ &\stackrel{(\star)}{=}\; \sum_x q(x)\sum_y q(y|x)\,f\!\bigl(\tfrac{p(x,y)}{q(x,y)}\bigr) \\ &\stackrel{(\dagger)}{\ge}\; \sum_x q(x)\,f\!\Bigl(\sum_y q(y|x)\tfrac{p(x,y)}{q(x,y)}\Bigr) \\ &= \sum_x q(x)\,f\!\bigl(\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}\bigr) \;=\; D_f(p\,\Vert\,q). \end{aligned}$$

$(\star)$ chain rule on $q$; $(\dagger)$ Jensen on convex $f$ against $q(y|x)$.

Proof — Part 3 (DPI)

Push $p,q$ through channel $p(y|x)$ to get marginals $p(y)=\sum_x p(x)p(y|x)$ and $q(y)$. Combine Parts 1 and 2:

$$\begin{aligned} D_f\!\bigl(p(y)\,\Vert\,q(y)\bigr) &\stackrel{(\text{Part 2})}{\le} D_f\!\bigl(p(x,y)\,\Vert\,q(x,y)\bigr) \\ &= D_f\!\bigl(p(x)p(y|x)\,\Vert\,q(x)p(y|x)\bigr) \\ &\stackrel{(\text{Part 1})}{=} D_f(p\,\Vert\,q). \end{aligned}$$

Processing cannot increase distinguishability under any $f$-divergence.

Proof — Part 4 (Information Inequality)

By Jensen and $\sum_x p(x) = 1$:

$$D_f(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; \sum_x q(x)\,f\!\bigl(\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}\bigr) \;\ge\; f\!\Bigl(\sum_x q(x)\,\tfrac{p(x)}{q(x)}\Bigr) \;=\; f(1) \;=\; 0.$$

If $f$ is strictly convex at $1$, equality in Jensen forces $p(x)/q(x)$ constant on $\mathrm{supp}(q)$; that constant must be $1$, so $p=q$.

Recap — Properties of $D_f$

All four properties follow from a single tool, Jensen on the convex $f$:

$$\begin{aligned} \text{Part 1 (joint = marginal)} &\;\Leftarrow\;\text{conditional cancels in } r=p/q, \\ \text{Part 2 (joint } \ge \text{ marginal)} &\;\Leftarrow\;\text{Jensen on } q(y|x), \\ \text{Part 3 (DPI)} &\;\Leftarrow\;\text{Parts 1 and 2}, \\ \text{Part 4 (info inequality)} &\;\Leftarrow\;\text{Jensen on } q(x). \end{aligned}$$

Every $f$-divergence inherits the structure of KL: cheap generalization, identical machinery.

04

GAN $\equiv$ Jensen–Shannon

A minimax game over $f$-divergence

Setup — Generative Adversarial Net

Two networks compete:

  • Generator $g_\theta$: samples $\tilde X = g_\theta(Z)$ for $Z\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$, induces $p_\theta$.
  • Discriminator $d_\phi : \mathcal{X}\to[0,1]$: estimates probability of "real".

The minimax value function:

$$V(g_\theta, d_\phi) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{p_{\mathrm{data}}}\!\bigl[\log d_\phi(X)\bigr] + \mathbb{E}_{p_\theta}\!\bigl[\log(1-d_\phi(X))\bigr].$$
Goodfellow et al., "Generative Adversarial Networks", NeurIPS 2014.

Reading the Minimax — Two Players

Discriminator $\max_{d_\phi} V$

Classify real vs fake.

$d_\phi(\text{real}) \to 1$,   $d_\phi(\text{fake}) \to 0$.

Generator $\min_{g_\theta} V$

Fool the discriminator.

$d_\phi(\text{fake}) \to 1$,   i.e. $p_\theta \to p_{\mathrm{data}}$.

Equilibrium: $d_\phi^\star \equiv 1/2$,   $p_\theta = p_{\mathrm{data}}$.

Overview — The JS Theorem

For any fixed generator $g_\theta$, the best discriminator picks

$$d_{\phi^*}(x) \;=\; \frac{p_{\mathrm{data}}(x)}{p_{\mathrm{data}}(x) + p_\theta(x)}.$$

Plugging $d_{\phi^*}$ back into $V$ gives exactly

$$V(g_\theta, d_{\phi^*}) \;=\; 2\,D_{\mathrm{JS}}(p_{\mathrm{data}}\,\Vert\,p_\theta) \;-\; 2\log 2.$$

Inner max is a divergence; outer min is JS minimization.

Theorem — GAN Optimal Value

Theorem (Goodfellow et al., 2014).

For any fixed generator $g_\theta$ inducing $p_\theta$,

$$\max_{d_\phi}\, V(g_\theta, d_\phi) \;=\; 2\,D_{\mathrm{JS}}(p_{\mathrm{data}}\,\Vert\,p_\theta) \;-\; 2\log 2.$$

Equivalently, the minimax problem $\min_\theta \max_\phi V(g_\theta, d_\phi)$ minimizes the Jensen–Shannon divergence between data and model.

Goodfellow, Pouget-Abadie, Mirza, Xu, Warde-Farley, Ozair, Courville, and Bengio, NeurIPS 2014.

Proof — Step 1 (Optimal Discriminator)

Write the value pointwise; integrand at $x$:

$$p_{\mathrm{data}}(x)\log d(x) + p_\theta(x)\log\bigl(1-d(x)\bigr).$$

Differentiate in $d\in(0,1)$, set to zero:

$$\frac{p_{\mathrm{data}}(x)}{d(x)} - \frac{p_\theta(x)}{1-d(x)} \;=\; 0 \;\;\Longrightarrow\;\; d_{\phi^*}(x) \;=\; \frac{p_{\mathrm{data}}(x)}{p_{\mathrm{data}}(x) + p_\theta(x)}.$$

Concavity of $a\log d + b\log(1-d)$ in $d$ confirms a maximum.

Proof — Step 2 (Substitute Back)

Write $m(x) = \tfrac{p_{\mathrm{data}}(x)+p_\theta(x)}{2}$. Then $1 - d_{\phi^*}(x) = \tfrac{p_\theta(x)}{p_{\mathrm{data}}(x)+p_\theta(x)}$, and

$$\begin{aligned} V(g_\theta, d_{\phi^*}) &= \mathbb{E}_{p_{\mathrm{data}}}\!\bigl[\log\tfrac{p_{\mathrm{data}}}{p_{\mathrm{data}}+p_\theta}\bigr] + \mathbb{E}_{p_\theta}\!\bigl[\log\tfrac{p_\theta}{p_{\mathrm{data}}+p_\theta}\bigr] \\ &= \mathbb{E}_{p_{\mathrm{data}}}\!\bigl[\log\tfrac{p_{\mathrm{data}}}{2m}\bigr] + \mathbb{E}_{p_\theta}\!\bigl[\log\tfrac{p_\theta}{2m}\bigr] \\ &= D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p_{\mathrm{data}}\,\Vert\,m) + D_{\mathrm{KL}}(p_\theta\,\Vert\,m) - 2\log 2. \end{aligned}$$

The first two terms are exactly $D_{\mathrm{JS}}(p_{\mathrm{data}}\,\Vert\,p_\theta)$. Hence $V(g_\theta, d_{\phi^*}) = 2\,D_{\mathrm{JS}} - 2\log 2$. $\quad\blacksquare$

Recap — What the Proof Says

$$\begin{aligned} \max_{d_\phi}\,V(g_\theta, d_\phi) &\stackrel{(1)}{=}\; \mathbb{E}_{p_{\mathrm{data}}}\!\bigl[\log\tfrac{p_{\mathrm{data}}}{p_{\mathrm{data}}+p_\theta}\bigr] + \mathbb{E}_{p_\theta}\!\bigl[\log\tfrac{p_\theta}{p_{\mathrm{data}}+p_\theta}\bigr] \\ &\stackrel{(2)}{=}\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(p_{\mathrm{data}}\,\Vert\,m\bigr) + D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(p_\theta\,\Vert\,m\bigr) - 2\log 2 \\ &\stackrel{(3)}{=}\; 2\,D_{\mathrm{JS}}(p_{\mathrm{data}}\,\Vert\,p_\theta) - 2\log 2. \end{aligned}$$
  • $(1)$: pointwise optimal $d^*=p_{\mathrm{data}}/(p_{\mathrm{data}}+p_\theta)$.
  • $(2)$: rewrite ratios against $m=(p_{\mathrm{data}}+p_\theta)/2$.
  • $(3)$: recognize the two KL terms as JS.

Training Algorithm

  1. Initialize $\phi^{(0)}, \theta^{(0)}$.
  2. For $t = 1,\dots,T$: sample $x^{(t)} \sim p_{\mathrm{data}}$ and $z^{(t)} \sim \mathcal{N}(0,I)$.
  3. Ascend $\phi$ on $\log d_\phi(x^{(t)}) + \log(1 - d_\phi(g_{\theta^{(t-1)}}(z^{(t)})))$.
  4. Descend $\theta$ on $\log\bigl(1 - d_{\phi^{(t)}}(g_\theta(z^{(t)}))\bigr)$.

Mini-batches in practice; alternation approximates the inner max.

Beyond JS — The $f$-GAN Family

Replacing $\log$ with another convex pair gives a different divergence. Each $f$-divergence has a variational (Fenchel-dual) representation

$$D_f(p\,\Vert\,q) \;=\; \sup_{T:\,\mathcal{X}\to\mathbb{R}} \;\mathbb{E}_p[T(X)] - \mathbb{E}_q[f^*(T(X))],$$

where $f^*(s) = \sup_t(st - f(t))$. Each choice of $f$ produces a different GAN: KL-GAN, $\chi^2$-GAN, Hellinger-GAN, …

Two roads from one machine. Setting $p = p_{\mathrm{data}}$, $q = p_\theta$ and minimizing in $\theta$ gives generative training. Setting $p = p_{X,Y}$, $q = p_X p_Y$ and maximizing the inner $T$ gives MI estimation — the $f = t\log t$ instance is exactly DV / NWJ (mi1 §03–04).
Nowozin, Cseke, and Tomioka, $f$-GAN, NeurIPS 2016.

The Catalogue, at a Glance

$$\begin{array}{l|c|c} \text{Divergence} & f(t) & \text{Where it appears} \\\hline \text{KL} & t\log t & \text{ML, ELBO, MI bounds} \\ \text{Reverse KL} & -\log t & \text{VI, mode-seeking} \\ \text{TV} & \tfrac12|t-1| & \text{Hypothesis testing} \\ \chi^2 & (t-1)^2 & \text{NWJ bound, } \chi^2\text{-GAN} \\ \text{Hockey-stick} & [t-\gamma]_+ & (\varepsilon,\delta)\text{-DP} \\ \text{JS} & t\log\tfrac{2t}{t+1}+\log\tfrac{2}{t+1} & \text{Vanilla GAN} \end{array}$$

One convex $f$, one definition, one set of properties.
