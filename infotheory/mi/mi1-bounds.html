01

Setup

Density ratios from samples alone

Why Estimate Mutual Information?

MI captures dependence that correlation misses — nonlinear, multimodal, high-dimensional.

  • CLIP: maximize $I(\text{image};\text{caption})$ over 400M web pairs — one shared embedding for vision and language.
  • Contrastive SSL: maximize $I(\text{view}_1;\text{view}_2)$ across augmentations (SimCLR, MoCo, BYOL).
  • Information bottleneck: learn $T$ with high $I(T;Y)$ and low $I(T;X)$.
  • Feature selection / independence testing: rank by $I(X_j;Y)$; $I = 0 \iff X \perp Y$.
Modern setting: $X,Y$ are images, text, embeddings — high-dimensional, no closed-form density.

MI as a KL Divergence

$$I(X;Y) \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(p_{X,Y}\,\Vert\,p_X p_Y\bigr) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{p_{X,Y}}\!\left[\log\dfrac{p(X,Y)}{p(X)p(Y)}\right].$$

The MI estimation problem reduces to: estimate a KL between two distributions, given only samples.

$$P \;=\; p_{X,Y}\quad\text{(joint)},\qquad Q \;=\; p_X p_Y\quad\text{(product of marginals)}.$$

Why Is It Hard?

The integrand is a density ratio — we have samples, not densities.

$$I(X;Y) \;=\; \int\!\!\int p(x,y)\,\log\frac{p(x,y)}{p(x)p(y)}\,dx\,dy.$$
  • Plug-in via KDE / $k$-NN density estimation: $O(n^{-2/(4+d)})$ rate — useless past $d \approx 5$.
  • CLIP embeddings live in $\mathbb{R}^{512}$. No hope of estimating $p(x,y)$ directly.
  • Even when $r$ is known, variance of any MC estimate grows as $\sim e^{I}/n$ (we return to this in §05).
Need: a sample-based, dimension-agnostic estimator that side-steps density estimation.

What We Can — and Cannot — Compute

Given iid pairs $(x_i, y_i) \sim p_{X,Y}$:

OperationAvailable?
Sample from joint $P = p_{X,Y}$✓  the data
Sample from product $Q = p_X p_Y$✓  shuffle $y$'s within a batch
Evaluate $H(X)$Sometimes — if marginal is discrete or analytic
Evaluate $p(x,y)$, $p(x)$, $p(y)$ pointwise×
Evaluate density ratio $r(x,y) = p(x,y)/p(x)p(y)$×

Density-Ratio Framing

Define

$$r(x,y) \;=\; \dfrac{p(x,y)}{p(x)p(y)}, \qquad I(X;Y) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{p_{X,Y}}[\log r(X,Y)].$$

If $r$ were known, MI is a single expectation under the joint. Strategy: train a critic $T_\theta \approx \log r$ by maximizing a variational lower bound on $I(X;Y)$.

02

Barber–Agakov

Variational lower bound via a conditional model $q(x\mid y)$

BA Bound

Theorem (Barber & Agakov, 2003).

For any conditional density $q(x\mid y)$:

$$I(X;Y) \;\ge\; H(X) + \mathbb{E}_{p(x,y)}[\log q(x\mid y)] \;=:\; I_{\mathrm{BA}}(q).$$

Tight when $q(x\mid y) = p(x\mid y)$.

Barber & Agakov, "The IM Algorithm: A Variational Approach to Information Maximization", NeurIPS 2003.

Proof — Add and Subtract $\log q$

$I(X;Y) = H(X) - H(X\mid Y)$ and $H(X\mid Y) = -\mathbb{E}\log p(x\mid y)$. Insert $\log q$:

$$H(X\mid Y) \;=\; -\mathbb{E}\log q(x\mid y) - \mathbb{E}_{p(y)}D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(p(\cdot\mid y)\,\Vert\,q(\cdot\mid y)\bigr).$$

Substitute back; KL non-negativity gives the bound. Gap is $\mathbb{E}_{p(y)}D_{\mathrm{KL}}(\cdot\Vert q)$, vanishing iff $q = p(\cdot\mid y)$.

BA in Practice — Reduces to MLE

$H(X)$ is constant in $q$:

$$\max_q I_{\mathrm{BA}}(q) \;=\; H(X) + \max_q\mathbb{E}_{p(x,y)}[\log q_\theta(x\mid y)].$$

Train representations

Maximize the conditional log-likelihood. No MI machinery needed.

Report MI numbers

Need $H(X)$ — typically unknown. BA is not a plug-in estimator.

03

Donsker–Varadhan

Dual KL representation, basis of MINE

DV Representation

Theorem (Donsker & Varadhan, 1983).

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,Q) \;=\; \sup_T\;\bigl\{\mathbb{E}_P[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]\bigr\}.$$

Sup over measurable $T$ with finite expectations.

Apply with $P = p_{X,Y}$, $Q = p_X p_Y$ to get a lower bound on MI.

DV Bound on MI

$$I(X;Y) \;\ge\; \mathbb{E}_{P}[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_{Q}[e^T] \;=:\; I_{\mathrm{DV}}(T).$$

Tight at $T^\star(x,y) = \log r(x,y) + c$ for any constant $c$.

Optimal critic is the log-density-ratio. Tightest possible bound when fully optimized.

Proof — Tilt $Q$ by $e^T$

Define $G$ via $\frac{dG}{dQ} = \frac{e^T}{\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]}$. Compute:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,G) \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,Q) - \mathbb{E}_P[T] + \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] \;\ge\; 0.$$

Rearrange. Equality iff $P = G$, i.e. $T = \log(dP/dQ) + c$.

MINE — Neural DV Estimator

Parameterize $T_\theta(x,y)$ as a neural net. Replace each expectation by a sample mean (Monte Carlo):

$$\widehat{I}_{\mathrm{DV}}(T_\theta) \;=\; \underbrace{\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} T_\theta(x_i, y_i)}_{\approx\,\mathbb{E}_P[T]} \;-\; \log\underbrace{\dfrac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} e^{T_\theta(x_i, y'_i)}}_{\approx\,\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]}.$$
  • Positives $(x_i,y_i) \sim p_{X,Y}$: data pairs → first average.
  • Negatives $(x_i,y'_i)$: shuffle $y$'s within the minibatch — same marginals, broken pairing → second average.

Gradient-ascend $\widehat{I}_{\mathrm{DV}}$ on $\theta$. Variance of the log-sum-exp term — revisited in §05.

Belghazi et al., "MINE: Mutual Information Neural Estimation", ICML 2018.
04

NWJ

Replace the log-partition with a linear surrogate

From DV to NWJ

For $u>0$: $\log u \le u - 1$. Apply with $u = \mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]$:

$$\log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] \;\le\; \mathbb{E}_Q[e^T] - 1.$$

Substitute into DV and reparameterize $T \to T+1$ to absorb the constant.

NWJ Bound

Theorem (Nguyen, Wainwright, Jordan, 2010).

$$I(X;Y) \;\ge\; \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \mathbb{E}_Q[e^{T-1}] \;=:\; I_{\mathrm{NWJ}}(T).$$

Tight at $T^\star = \log r + 1$.

Nguyen et al., "Estimating Divergence Functionals and the Likelihood Ratio", IEEE Trans. IT 2010.

NWJ vs. DV — Variance Trade

DV

Log-partition is volatile: one large $T$ dominates. Variance $\sim e^I/N$.

NWJ

Replaces $\log\mathbb{E}[e^T]$ with $\mathbb{E}[e^{T-1}]$. Stable, but generally looser at finite-batch optima.

Same optimal critic, different finite-sample behavior.
05

Tradeoffs & Unification

High MI barrier; $f$-divergence variational view

The High-MI Barrier

For strongly dependent pairs, $\log r$ has a long right tail. McAllester & Stratos (2020):

$$\operatorname{Var}\!\bigl[\widehat{\log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]}\bigr] \;\sim\; \dfrac{e^{I(X;Y)}}{N}.$$

To estimate $I = 10$ nats: need $N\sim e^{10}\approx 22{,}000$ samples for variance control alone.

No sample-efficient estimator of high MI. Information-theoretic barrier, not a software bug.

Three Bounds Side by Side

BoundOptimumTrade
BA$q = p(\cdot\mid y)$Reduces to MLE; needs $H(X)$
DV$T = \log r + c$Tightest; variance $\sim e^I/N$
NWJ$T = \log r + 1$Stable; looser at fixed critic class

All three share the same optimal critic; differ in finite-sample behavior.

Choosing Your Bound

  • Optimization only: BA. Reduces to MLE.
  • Stable contrastive objective: InfoNCE (next lecture). Bounded by $\log K$.
  • Tight numerical estimate at low/moderate MI: NWJ or DV with care.
  • High MI ($I\gg 1$ nat): no estimator is reliable; report an interval.

One Machine — KL Is the Tip of It

Every $f$-divergence has a Fenchel-dual variational form. Substitute $f(t) = t\log t$, conjugate $f^*(s) = e^{s-1}$:

$$\underbrace{\mathbb{E}_P[T] - \mathbb{E}_Q[e^{T-1}]}_{\text{NWJ (bare conjugate)}} \;\;\le\;\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}(P\,\Vert\,Q) \;\;=\;\; \sup_T\bigl\{\underbrace{\mathbb{E}_P[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]}_{\text{DV (tighten over constants)}}\bigr\}.$$

Other $f$ give $\chi^2$-, JS-, hockey-stick-MI bounds. Derivation and $f$-GAN: div1 §04.

Recap

  • Setup: samples from $P = p_{X,Y}$ and $Q = p_X p_Y$.
  • BA: $I\ge H(X) + \mathbb{E}\log q(x\mid y)$ — MLE in disguise.
  • DV: $I\ge \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \log\mathbb{E}_Q[e^T]$ — tight, MINE-class variance.
  • NWJ: $I\ge \mathbb{E}_P[T] - \mathbb{E}_Q[e^{T-1}]$ — stable, slightly looser.
  • Unified view: all three are KL instances of the $f$-divergence variational bound.
  • Barrier: variance $\sim e^I/N$. Reliable only at low-to-moderate MI.
