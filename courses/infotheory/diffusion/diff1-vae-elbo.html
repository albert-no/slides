01

Why a Bound

Likelihood is intractable; we minimize a surrogate

Latent-Variable Model

Prior $p(z)=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$. Decoder $p_\theta(x\mid z)$.

$$p_\theta(x) = \int p(z)\,p_\theta(x\mid z)\,dz.$$

Sampling is easy: draw $z\sim p(z)$, then $x\sim p_\theta(x\mid z)$.

Computing $p_\theta(x)$ is not.

Maximum Likelihood Is Blocked

Average negative log-likelihood:

$$\theta^\star \in \arg\min_\theta \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N -\log p_\theta(x_i).$$

Each $\log p_\theta(x_i) = \log\!\int p_\theta(x_i, z)\,dz$: integral over $z$, not closed form.

Replace the intractable log-marginal with a tractable lower bound.

Plan: A Variational Posterior

Pick an encoder family $q_\phi(z\mid x)$, tractable, parameterized by $\phi$.

Importance-sampling identity:

$$p_\theta(x) = \mathbb{E}_{z\sim q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)}\!\left[\frac{p_\theta(x,z)}{q_\phi(z\mid x)}\right].$$

Take $\log$. Two ways to bound it.

02

ELBO: Two Paths, One Bound

Jensen and the KL identity

Path 1: Jensen on $\log\mathbb{E}$

Concavity of $\log$ gives $\log\mathbb{E}[X]\ge\mathbb{E}[\log X]$. Apply to the importance ratio:

$$\log p_\theta(x) \ge \mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}\!\left[\log\frac{p_\theta(x,z)}{q_\phi(z\mid x)}\right] \;=:\; \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}(\theta,\phi;x).$$

Tight when $\log$ acts linearly on the ratio. Gap is opaque.

Path 2: KL Identity

Bayes: $p_\theta(z\mid x) = p_\theta(x,z)/p_\theta(x)$. Multiply, divide, take expectation:

$$\log p_\theta(x) = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}(\theta,\phi;x) + D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)\,\Vert\,p_\theta(\cdot\mid x)\bigr).$$

$D_{\mathrm{KL}}\ge 0$ recovers the bound. The gap has a name: posterior KL.

Same Bound, Different Stories

$$\log p_\theta(x) \;=\; \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} \;+\; \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q_\phi\,\Vert\,p_\theta(\cdot\mid x)\bigr)}_{\ge\,0}.$$

Jensen

Gap from concavity. Vanishes when ratio is constant in $z$.

KL identity

Gap is posterior KL. Vanishes iff $q_\phi = p_\theta(\cdot\mid x)$.

Closing the gap means the encoder matches the true posterior.
03

What the Bound Asks

Reconstruction minus rate

Two Seams of the Joint

Both forms below are the same ELBO, opened along different factorizations of $p_\theta(x,z)$:

$$\begin{array}{l|c|c} \text{Split } p_\theta(x,z) \text{ as} & \text{Gives} & \text{Purpose} \\\hline p_\theta(z\mid x)\cdot p_\theta(x) & \text{KL gap identity} & \text{names the gap} \\ p(z)\cdot p_\theta(x\mid z) & \text{recon} - \text{rate} & \text{trainable loss} \end{array}$$
Same bound, two algebraic moves. Path 2 names the gap; the next slide gives the loss.

ELBO Decomposes

Expand $p_\theta(x,z) = p(z)\,p_\theta(x\mid z)$ inside the ELBO:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} \;=\; \underbrace{\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)]}_{\text{reconstruction}} \;-\; \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)\,\Vert\,p(\cdot)\bigr)}_{\text{rate}}.$$

Two competing pressures: explain $x$, but stay close to the prior.

Reconstruction — What It Computes

Encoder maps $x \mapsto z$. Decoder outputs $p_\theta(\cdot\mid z)$. Evaluate on the original $x$:

$$\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi(z\mid x)}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)].$$

How much probability does the decoder place on the true $x$, given the latent $z$?

Gaussian Decoder — Reconstruction Is MSE

For $p_\theta(x\mid z) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta(z),\,\sigma^2 I)$:

$$\log p_\theta(x\mid z) \;=\; -\frac{\|x - \mu_\theta(z)\|^2}{2\sigma^2} + \mathrm{const}.$$
Maximizing reconstruction $=$ minimizing MSE between input and decoded output.

Rate vs. Distortion

Reconstruction

Can the decoder recover $x$ from the latent $z$?

Negative distortion (MSE).

Rate (prior KL)

How far does the encoder drift from $p(z)$?

Bits the latent carries about $x$.

ELBO is a variational rate–distortion functional.

Why Prior KL $=$ Rate

Average the KL-to-prior over $x\sim p_{\mathrm{data}}$:

$$\mathbb{E}_x\!\bigl[D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)\,\Vert\,p)\bigr] \;=\; \underbrace{I_{q_\phi}(X;Z)}_{\text{rate (bits)}} \;+\; \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q_\phi(z)\,\Vert\,p(z)\bigr)}_{\text{marginal mismatch}}.$$

$I(X;Z)$: bits the latent carries about data $=$ rate in the R-D sense.

Prior KL $\ge I(X;Z)$, tight when aggregate posterior $q_\phi(z) = p(z)$.

04

The VAE

Instantiating the ELBO with concrete choices

From ELBO to Algorithm

We have a loss. To train it, three design choices remain:

$$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} \;=\; \underbrace{\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)]}_{\text{need gradient through } q_\phi} \;-\; \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi\,\Vert\,p)}_{\text{need closed form}}.$$
  • Encoder $q_\phi(z\mid x)$: what family? → Gaussian
  • KL term: analytic or Monte Carlo? → analytic (Gaussian vs Gaussian)
  • Gradient through sampling: how? → reparameterization trick

VAE Architecture

Encoder

$x$
$\to$
$q_\phi$
$\to$
$z$

$z \sim \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\mu_\phi(x),\,\sigma_\phi(x)^2\bigr)$

Mean and std are functions of $x$.

Decoder

$z$
$\to$
$p_\theta$
$\to$
$\hat x$

Reconstructs $x$ from a sampled $z$.

Gaussian Encoder

For $z\in\mathbb{R}^d$, network outputs per-coordinate mean and variance:

$$q_\phi(z\mid x) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\mu_\phi(x),\,\operatorname{diag}\sigma_\phi(x)^2\bigr).$$

$\mu_{\phi,j},\,\sigma_{\phi,j}$: $j$-th coordinates of $\mu_\phi(x),\,\sigma_\phi(x)$, for $j=1,\dots,d$.

Gaussian choice $\Rightarrow$ closed-form KL and reparameterization.

Closed-Form KL

Prior $p(z) = \mathcal{N}(0,I)$. KL from encoder to prior:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q_\phi\,\Vert\,p\bigr) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2}\sum_{j=1}^{d}\!\bigl(\mu_{\phi,j}^2 + \sigma_{\phi,j}^2 - 1 - \log\sigma_{\phi,j}^2\bigr).$$

Each term $= 0$ iff $\mu_{\phi,j} = 0$ and $\sigma_{\phi,j} = 1$.

Rate term is analytic — no Monte Carlo.

The Gradient Problem

Reconstruction requires differentiating through a sample:

$$\nabla_\phi\;\mathbb{E}_{z\sim q_\phi(z\mid x)}[\log p_\theta(x\mid z)] \;=\; \text{?}$$

$z$ is drawn from $q_\phi$. The sampling distribution depends on $\phi$.

Cannot move $\nabla_\phi$ inside $\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}$ directly.

Reparameterization Trick

Separate randomness from parameters:

$$z = \mu_\phi(x) + \sigma_\phi(x)\odot\varepsilon, \qquad \varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I).$$

Now $\varepsilon$ is parameter-free. Rewrite the expectation:

$$\nabla_\phi\;\mathbb{E}_{q_\phi}[f(z)] \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{\varepsilon}\!\bigl[\nabla_\phi\, f\!\bigl(\mu_\phi(x)+\sigma_\phi(x)\odot\varepsilon\bigr)\bigr].$$
All $\phi$-dependence flows through $\mu_\phi, \sigma_\phi$. Standard backprop applies.

VAE Loss = Negative ELBO

Per sample $x$, one $\varepsilon$ draw gives a single-sample estimate:

$$\mathcal{L}(\theta,\phi;x) \;=\; \underbrace{-\log p_\theta(x\mid z)}_{\text{reconstruction (MSE)}} \;+\; \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q_\phi(\cdot\mid x)\,\Vert\,p\bigr)}_{\text{rate (closed form)}}.$$

First term: Monte Carlo (one $\varepsilon$). Second term: analytic.

Backprop through both encoder and decoder jointly.

Failure Mode: Posterior Collapse

If the decoder can fit $x$ without help from $z$, the encoder has no reason to encode $x$:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi\,\Vert\,p) = 0 \;\Longleftrightarrow\; q_\phi(z\mid x) = p(z) \;\Longrightarrow\; z\perp x.$$

Latent carries no information. Mitigations: KL annealing, free bits, weaker decoder.

Diffusion sidesteps this: encoder is fixed, $z$ is forced to carry $x$.

Recap

  • Latent model: $p_\theta(x)=\int p(z)\,p_\theta(x\mid z)\,dz$, intractable.
  • ELBO: $\log p_\theta(x) = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} + D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_\phi\Vert p_\theta(\cdot\mid x))$.
  • Decomposition: reconstruction (MSE) minus rate (KL to prior).
  • VAE: Gaussian encoder, reparameterization, joint backprop.
  • Collapse: learned encoder can drop $x$; diffusion freezes it.
