01

$(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP

A small failure probability enables Gaussian noise and tighter bounds.

Approximate Differential Privacy

Definition ($(\varepsilon,\delta)$-DP, Dwork et al. 2006).

$\mathcal{M}: \mathcal{X}^n \to \mathcal{Y}$ is $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP if for every neighboring $D, D'$ and every measurable $O \subseteq \mathcal{Y}$,

$$\Pr[\mathcal{M}(D) \in O] \;\le\; e^{\varepsilon}\, \Pr[\mathcal{M}(D') \in O] + \delta.$$
  • $\delta = 0$: pure $\varepsilon$-DP (Lecture 2).
  • $\delta > 0$ (e.g. $10^{-6}$): approximate DP — slack for tail events.
  • $\delta$ should be cryptographically small (e.g. $\ll 1/n$).

Interpretations

  • Membership inference. An adversary's odds ratio is bounded by $e^\varepsilon$ except with probability $\delta$.
  • Hypothesis testing. For $H_0$: individual $i$ in $D$ vs $H_1$: $i$ replaced — log-likelihood ratio $\le \varepsilon$ except for a $\delta$-mass event.
  • Worst-case. Holds for every $(D, D')$ pair and every event $O$.
$\delta$ allows a small "catastrophic event" probability — must be controlled.

Privacy-Loss Random Variable

Definition.

For neighbors $X, X'$, the privacy loss at output $y$ is

$$I_{X, X'}(y) \;=\; \log \frac{\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) = y]}{\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X') = y]}.$$

The privacy-loss random variable is $I_{X, X'}(Y)$ with $Y = \mathcal{M}(X)$.

$\mathcal{M}$ is $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP iff a controlled tail bound on $I_{X, X'}(Y)$ holds for all neighboring $(X, X')$.

Standard Proof Technique — Good/Bad Split

Partition the output space:

$$G = \{y \in \mathcal{Y} : I_{X, X'}(y) \le \varepsilon\}, \qquad B = \mathcal{Y} \setminus G.$$

For any $S \subseteq \mathcal{Y}$:

$$\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in S] = \Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in S \cap G] + \Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in S \cap B] \;\le\; e^{\varepsilon}\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X') \in S] + \Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in B].$$

It suffices to show $\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in B] \le \delta$, i.e. tail of $I_{X, X'}$ is $\le \delta$.

02

Truncated Laplace

A bounded variant of the Laplace mechanism.

Truncated Laplacian Mechanism

Define $Z \sim \mathrm{LapTr}(\lambda, \tau)$ with pdf

$$f_Z(z) \;=\; \begin{cases} \tfrac{1}{Z_0}\, e^{-|z|/\lambda} & |z| \le \tau \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

The mechanism: $\mathcal{M}(X) = f(X) + (Z_1, \dots, Z_d)$ with $Z_i \overset{\text{iid}}\sim \mathrm{LapTr}(\Delta/\varepsilon, \tau)$ for appropriate $\tau$.

  • Utility is comparable to standard Laplace.
  • Gives a worst-case bound on noise magnitude — important when downstream code cannot handle outliers.
03

Gaussian Mechanism

Gaussian noise on $\ell_2$-sensitivity — the workhorse of DP-ML.

Gaussian Mechanism

Definition.

For $f : \mathcal{X}^n \to \mathbb{R}^d$ with $\ell_2$-sensitivity $\Delta_2 = \max_{D, D'} \|f(D) - f(D')\|_2$, release

$$\mathcal{M}(D) \;=\; f(D) + \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I), \qquad \sigma \ge \frac{\Delta_2\, \sqrt{2 \ln(1.25/\delta)}}{\varepsilon}.$$

Theorem.

The Gaussian mechanism with this $\sigma$ is $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP.

Dwork & Roth, "Algorithmic Foundations of Differential Privacy", 2014.

Proof — 1-D Privacy Loss

1-D case. Let $K = f(X) - f(X')$ with $|K| \le \Delta$. Privacy loss at output $y$:

$$I_{X, X'}(y) = \log\frac{\exp(-(y - f(X))^2 / 2\sigma^2)}{\exp(-(y - f(X'))^2 / 2\sigma^2)} = \frac{(y - f(X'))^2 - (y - f(X))^2}{2\sigma^2}.$$

Expanding the squares:

$$I_{X, X'}(y) \;=\; \frac{K\,(K + 2(y - f(X)))}{2\sigma^2}.$$

Under $\mathcal{M}(X) = f(X) + \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2)$, $(Y - f(X))/\sigma \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1)$.

Proof — Tail Bound on Privacy Loss

Use $\Pr[\mathcal{N}(0, 1) \ge t] \le e^{-t^2/2}$:

$$\begin{aligned} \Pr[I_{X, X'}(Y) > \varepsilon] &= \Pr\!\Bigl[\frac{Y - f(X)}{\sigma} > \frac{\varepsilon\sigma}{K} - \frac{K}{2\sigma}\Bigr] \\ &\le \exp\!\Bigl(-\tfrac12 \bigl(\tfrac{\varepsilon\sigma}{\Delta} - \tfrac{\Delta}{2\sigma}\bigr)^2\Bigr) \\ &\le \exp\!\Bigl(-\tfrac12 \tfrac{\varepsilon^2\sigma^2}{\Delta^2}\Bigr) \;\le\; \delta. \end{aligned}$$

The last step uses $\sigma \ge \Delta \sqrt{2\ln(1.25/\delta)}/\varepsilon$. Combine with the good/bad split ⇒ $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP. $\blacksquare$

Multi-Dim — Useful Lemma

Lemma.

If $Z = (Z_1, \dots, Z_d) \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I)$, then for any $v \in \mathbb{R}^d$, $v \cdot Z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 \|v\|^2)$.

This reduces the multi-dimensional privacy loss to a 1-D Gaussian — exactly the situation handled above.

Proof — Multi-Dim Privacy Loss

Let $v = f(X) - f(X') \in \mathbb{R}^d$ with $\|v\|_2 \le \Delta_2$. For $Y = f(X) + \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I)$:

$$I_{X, X'}(y) = \frac{\|y - f(X')\|^2 - \|y - f(X)\|^2}{2\sigma^2} = \frac{\|v\|^2}{2\sigma^2} + \frac{v \cdot (y - f(X))}{\sigma^2}.$$

By the lemma, $v \cdot (Y - f(X)) \sim \mathcal{N}(0, \|v\|^2 \sigma^2)$. The 1-D analysis then bounds the tail by $\delta$. $\blacksquare$

$\ell_2$ vs $\ell_1$ Sensitivity

For $d$ binary queries, $f(X) \in \{0, 1\}^d$:

$\ell_1$ sensitivity

$\Delta_1 = d$ — one record can change every answer.

$\ell_2$ sensitivity

$\Delta_2 = \sqrt d$ — combining the per-coordinate changes in $L^2$.

Gaussian noise calibrated to $\Delta_2$ uses $\sqrt{d}$-scale vs the $d$-scale required by Laplace — Gaussian wins in high dimension.
04

Properties of Approximate DP

Post-processing, composition, group privacy, subsampling — restated for $(\varepsilon,\delta)$.

Post-Processing for $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP

Theorem.

If $\mathcal{M}$ is $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP and $f$ is any (possibly randomized) function, $f \circ \mathcal{M}$ is also $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP.

Proof.

For neighbors $D, D'$ and any $O' \subseteq \mathcal{Y}'$,

$$\Pr[f(\mathcal{M}(D)) \in O'] = \Pr[\mathcal{M}(D) \in f^{-1}(O')] \le e^\varepsilon \Pr[\mathcal{M}(D') \in f^{-1}(O')] + \delta = e^\varepsilon \Pr[f(\mathcal{M}(D')) \in O'] + \delta. \;\blacksquare$$

Basic Composition for $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP

Theorem.

If $\mathcal{M}_1, \mathcal{M}_2$ are $(\varepsilon_1, \delta_1)$- and $(\varepsilon_2, \delta_2)$-DP respectively, then $(\mathcal{M}_1, \mathcal{M}_2)$ is $(\varepsilon_1 + \varepsilon_2,\, \delta_1 + \delta_2)$-DP.

  • $k$-fold: $(\sum_i \varepsilon_i,\, \sum_i \delta_i)$-DP. Linear in $k$.
  • Advanced composition (Lecture 5) replaces $k\varepsilon$ with $\sqrt{2 k \ln(1/\delta')}\,\varepsilon$ at the cost of an extra $\delta'$.

Group Privacy for $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP

Theorem.

If $\mathcal{M}$ is $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP, it is $(t\varepsilon,\; \tfrac{e^{t\varepsilon} - 1}{e^{\varepsilon} - 1} \delta)$-DP for any group of $t$ individuals.

For small $\varepsilon$, the $\delta$ factor is $\approx t\delta$.

Iterate the single-record composition $t$ times; both privacy parameters grow with $t$.

Subsampling Amplification for $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP

Apply Poisson subsampling with rate $q$ before $\mathcal{M}$ (each user included i.i.d. with prob $q$). If $\mathcal{M}$ is $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP, then $\mathcal{M}'$ is $(\varepsilon', \delta')$-DP with

$$\varepsilon' \;=\; \ln(1 + q(e^\varepsilon - 1)), \qquad \delta' \;=\; q\,\delta.$$
  • Pure DP ($\delta = 0$) ⇒ subsampled mechanism remains pure DP.
  • For small $q, \varepsilon$: $\varepsilon' \approx q\varepsilon$, $\delta' = q\delta$.

From Single-Step to Many-Step Accounting

Exact formulas above bound one application of a subsampled mechanism.

For repeated applications (e.g. DP-SGD training over thousands of steps), use:

  • Moments Accountant [Abadi et al. 2016]: tracks higher moments of the privacy-loss RV.
  • Rényi DP [Mironov 2017]: composition becomes additive in $\varepsilon$ at fixed Rényi order $\alpha$ (Lecture 6).
These tools achieve the tight $\sqrt T$ scaling needed for practical DP-SGD.

Recap

  • $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP: additive slack $\delta$ controls the tail of the privacy-loss RV.
  • Good/bad split is the standard proof skeleton: bound $\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in B] \le \delta$.
  • Gaussian mechanism: $\sigma = \Delta_2 \sqrt{2 \ln(1.25/\delta)}/\varepsilon$ yields $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP; preferred in high $d$.
  • Properties: post-processing, composition, group, subsampling — all carry over with appropriate $\delta$ scaling.
