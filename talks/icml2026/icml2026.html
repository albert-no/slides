ICML 2026 · Position Paper

The Term “Machine Unlearning”
Is Overused in LLMs

A call for terminology tied to guarantees

Sangyeon Yoon  ·  Yeachan Jun  ·  Albert No

Yonsei University  ·  ICML 2026

One Word, Many Meanings

LLM deletion requests are everywhere: privacy, copyright, safety, policy.

The literature labels all of them “unlearning.”

Same label

Refusal  ·  entity removal  ·  output suppression  ·  representation obfuscation  ·  knowledge editing.

Different guarantees

Indistinguishability from retraining vs. non-disclosure vs. behavior change.

Position. Reserve “machine unlearning” for dataset-defined deletion; everything else needs a different name.

What “Unlearning” Should Mean

Training set $D$, forget set $F \subset D$, retain set $R = D \setminus F$.

An unlearning procedure produces $\Theta' \leftarrow \mathrm{Unlearn}(\Theta_D, F)$.

$$\mathrm{Dist}\!\left(\mathcal{L}(\Theta'),\; \mathcal{L}(\Theta_R)\right) \;\le\; \tau, \qquad \Theta_R \sim \mathrm{Train}(R).$$

Baseline: retraining on $R$.   Target: indistinguishability, not non-disclosure.

Izzo et al., AISTATS 2021  ·  Ginart et al., NeurIPS 2019  ·  Guo et al., ICML 2020.

What Today’s Papers Actually Do

Output suppression

GA, NPO — drive likelihood on forget targets down.

Representation obfuscation

Distort internal activations on probe queries.

Knowledge editing

Rewrite specific facts or associations.

Behavioral refusal

IDK templates, LUNAR, DPO-style abstention.

All useful. None match retrain-indistinguishability.

Benchmarks Reward Surface Suppression

Common scores: lower ROUGE on forget queries, lower QA accuracy, lower likelihood.

None of these compare to a retrained reference.

  • TOFU — uses a retrained reference. Some follow-ups drop it.
  • MUSE / RWKU / WMDP — score output failure on probes.
  • Refusal beats deletion on these scores.

Optimizing the benchmark $\ne$ removing training influence.

The Hidden Gap: Derived Capabilities

Training on $F$ can induce transferable behavior, not just memorized strings.

Synthetic reasoning traces

Refusing the originals leaves the reasoning skill intact.

Data poisoning

Backdoors and biases persist after surface forgetting.

A retrained model would lose these capabilities; a suppressed model keeps them.

Pawelczyk et al., ICLR 2025  ·  Thaker et al., 2025  ·  Jia et al., 2025.

Surface Success Breaks Easily

Methods that pass output-failure metrics often fail under perturbation.

  • Paraphrase / multilingual probes recover “forgotten” content.
  • Benign fine-tuning on unrelated data un-suppresses.
  • Quantization alone can re-expose targets.

Training influence is still there — just hidden by the prompting protocol.

Maini et al., COLM 2024  ·  Lynch et al., 2024  ·  Łucki et al., TMLR 2025  ·  Zhang et al., 2025  ·  Yoon et al., 2026.

Call for Action

When a paper claims unlearning, report:

  • A reference model with provenance — $\mathrm{Train}(D \setminus F)$ or a stated proxy.
  • Distributional distances to that reference, not just forget-query scores.
  • Derived-capability probes — held-out tasks that test transferable skills.
  • Intervention-based recovery — benign fine-tuning, quantization, paraphrase.

For policy-driven goals, name the goal: suppression, refusal, editing.

Our Position, Restated

“Machine unlearning”  =  retrain-indistinguishability for a precisely defined forget set.

Other “forgetting” goals are valuable — but they are different problems and need different terms.

Without this distinction, benchmarks reward what is easy to measure, not what we set out to achieve.
