01

Forward Chain

Frozen encoder turning data into Gaussian noise

Setup: Variance-Preserving Steps

Schedule $\{\beta_t\}_{t=1}^T \subset (0,1)$. Set $\alpha_t = 1-\beta_t$, $\bar\alpha_t = \prod_{s\le t}\alpha_s$.

$$q(x_t\mid x_{t-1}) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\alpha_t}\,x_{t-1},\; \beta_t I\bigr).$$

Each step contracts toward 0 and adds Gaussian noise.

Variance-preserving: unit variance in, unit variance out.

Closed Form: $q(x_t\mid x_0)$

Lemma.

$$q(x_t\mid x_0) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0,\;(1-\bar\alpha_t)I\bigr).$$

Induct: mean $\sqrt{\alpha_t\bar\alpha_{t-1}}x_0 = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0$; variance $\alpha_t(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})+\beta_t = 1-\bar\alpha_t$.

Master Reparameterization

For $\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$:

$$x_t \;=\; \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon.$$

Jump from $(x_0, t)$ straight to $x_t$: no chain simulation in training.

As $t\to T$, $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$, so $x_T\approx\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.

02

Reverse Chain

Learned Gaussian kernels run the chain backwards

Generative Model

Start from prior $p(x_T)=\mathcal{N}(0,I)$, then reverse step by step:

$$p_\theta(x_{0:T}) \;=\; p(x_T)\prod_{t=1}^T p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t), \qquad p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t) = \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\mu_\theta(x_t,t),\,\Sigma_t\bigr).$$

Sampling: draw $x_T$, then $x_{T-1},\ldots,x_0$ via the learned kernels.

VAE: $x \to z \to \hat x$  (one latent).

Diffusion: $T$ Gaussian latents $x_1,\ldots,x_T$.

Encoder is fixed $\Rightarrow$ posterior collapse impossible.
03

ELBO Decomposition

$L_T + \sum L_{t-1} + L_0$: what the model is asked to do

Apply Lecture 1 to the Hierarchy

Lecture 1 ELBO with chain factorizations $p_\theta(x_{0:T})=p(x_T)\prod_t p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$ and $q(x_{1:T}\mid x_0)=\prod_t q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})$:

$$-\log p_\theta(x_0) \;\le\; \mathbb{E}_q\!\left[-\log p(x_T) \;+\; \sum_{t=1}^{T}\log\frac{q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})}{p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)}\right].$$

Forward $q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})$ has no $x_0$. Markov $\Rightarrow$ condition on $x_0$ is free; then Bayes flips the direction:

$$q(x_t\mid x_{t-1}) \;=\; q(x_t\mid x_{t-1}, x_0) \;=\; \dfrac{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0)\,q(x_t\mid x_0)}{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)}.$$

Split and Telescope

For $t\ge 2$, substitute Bayes and split each log:

$$\log\frac{q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})}{p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)} \;=\; \underbrace{\log\frac{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0)}{p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)}}_{\to\,L_{t-1}} \;+\; \log\frac{q(x_t\mid x_0)}{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)}.$$

Sum the right ratio across $t=2,\ldots,T$ — it telescopes:

$$\sum_{t=2}^{T}\log\frac{q(x_t\mid x_0)}{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)} \;=\; \log\frac{q(x_T\mid x_0)}{q(x_1\mid x_0)}.$$

Collect into Three Terms

Combine the telescoped log with $-\log p(x_T)$ to form $L_T$; the $t=1$ boundary becomes $L_0$; the per-step KLs collect into $\sum L_{t-1}$:

$$-\log p_\theta(x_0) \;\le\; L_T \;+\; \sum_{t=2}^{T} L_{t-1} \;+\; L_0,$$
  • $L_T = D_{\mathrm{KL}}\bigl(q(x_T\mid x_0)\,\Vert\,p(x_T)\bigr)$ — no $\theta$.
  • $L_{t-1} = \mathbb{E}_{q(x_t\mid x_0)}\,D_{\mathrm{KL}}\bigl(q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)\,\Vert\,p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)\bigr)$.
  • $L_0 = -\mathbb{E}_{q(x_1\mid x_0)}\log p_\theta(x_0\mid x_1)$.

$L_T$: Prior Matching (No Training)

$$L_T \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(x_T\mid x_0)\,\Vert\,p(x_T)\bigr) \;=\; D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_T}\,x_0,\,(1-\bar\alpha_T)I)\,\Vert\,\mathcal{N}(0,I)\bigr).$$

No $\theta$: entirely determined by the noise schedule.

As $T\to\infty$, $\bar\alpha_T\to 0$, so $q(x_T\mid x_0)\to\mathcal{N}(0,I) = p(x_T)$ and $L_T\to 0$.

$L_0$: Final Reconstruction

$$L_0 \;=\; -\mathbb{E}_{q(x_1\mid x_0)}\!\bigl[\log p_\theta(x_0\mid x_1)\bigr].$$

Decoder log-likelihood of the clean data given the least-noisy latent $x_1$.

In practice: small relative to $\sum L_{t-1}$; absorbed into the training loss.

All training signal lives in the interior terms $L_1, \ldots, L_{T-1}$.

Interior Term $L_{t-1}$ Is the Whole Game

$L_T$ has no $\theta$; $L_0$ is a boundary term. Only $L_{t-1}$ carries training signal:

$$L_{t-1} \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{q(x_t\mid x_0)}\,D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(\underbrace{q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0)}_{\text{true posterior — target}}\,\Vert\,\underbrace{p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)}_{\text{model}}\bigr).$$

Model is Gaussian by design. To evaluate the KL we need the true posterior $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0)$ in closed form too.

Derive $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0)$ — the DDPM target — in Section 04.
04

The DDPM Target

Closed-form $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)$ turns KL into MSE

Closed Form by Complete-the-Square

Rearrange Bayes (Sec 03) to isolate the target: $q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0) \propto q(x_t\mid x_{t-1})\,q(x_{t-1}\mid x_0)$. Both factors Gaussian in $x_{t-1}$ ⇒ product Gaussian.

Lemma (DDPM reverse conditional).

$$q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t, x_0) \;=\; \mathcal{N}\!\bigl(\tilde\mu_t(x_t,x_0),\,\tilde\beta_t I\bigr),$$
$$\tilde\beta_t = \dfrac{1-\bar\alpha_{t-1}}{1-\bar\alpha_t}\beta_t, \quad \tilde\mu_t = \dfrac{\sqrt{\alpha_t}(1-\bar\alpha_{t-1})}{1-\bar\alpha_t}x_t + \dfrac{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_{t-1}}\beta_t}{1-\bar\alpha_t}x_0.$$

$\tilde\mu_t$: weighted average of $x_t$ and $x_0$; $\tilde\beta_t$ shrinks as $\bar\alpha_{t-1}\to 0$. Algebra in the note.

$L_{t-1}$ Becomes MSE on the Mean

Plug the closed form into $L_{t-1}$. Pin both covariances to $\tilde\beta_t I$. KL between same-covariance Gaussians:

$$D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(\mathcal{N}(\tilde\mu_t,\tilde\beta_t I)\,\Vert\,\mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta,\tilde\beta_t I)\bigr) \;=\; \tfrac{1}{2\tilde\beta_t}\,\|\tilde\mu_t - \mu_\theta\|^2.$$
Training reduces to: predict the posterior mean $\tilde\mu_t(x_t, x_0)$ from $(x_t, t)$.

Three Ways to Parameterize $\mu_\theta$

  • Predict $x_0$: interpretable target.
  • Predict noise $\varepsilon$: DDPM default; well-conditioned across $t$.
  • Predict score $\nabla_x\log p_t(x)$: SDE perspective.
Lecture 3: derive the equivalences via Tweedie.

Recap

  • Forward: $q(x_t\mid x_0)=\mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}x_0,(1-\bar\alpha_t)I)$.
  • Reverse: learned Gaussian kernels $p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)$.
  • Hierarchical VAE: fixed encoder, deepest latent matched to $\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.
  • True reverse: Gaussian, $\mathcal{N}(\tilde\mu_t, \tilde\beta_t I)$, closed form.
  • ELBO: $L_T + \sum_{t=2}^{T} L_{t-1} + L_0$; interior terms are MSE on the mean.
