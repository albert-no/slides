01

Why Memorization Matters

Lawsuits, privacy, the central question

The Central Question

Model $f_\theta$ trained on corpus $\mathcal{D}$, sequence $x \in \mathcal{D}$:

Can a query recover $x$ verbatim?

Memorization: training data resurfacing through prompts.

Distinct from generalization. Distinct from membership inference.

Three Stakeholders

Privacy

PII, medical, secrets in training corpora.

Copyright

Books, code, news verbatim from a prompt.

Security

Canaries, credentials, system prompts leak.

Memorization is the empirical signature of all three risks.

Live Lawsuits (2023–2026)

  • NYT v. OpenAI — verbatim article reproduction from prompts
  • Authors Guild v. OpenAI — copyrighted books
  • Getty v. Stability AI — image reproduction with watermark
  • Disney v. Midjourney — character likeness

Plaintiffs cite verbatim reproductions as evidence of infringement.

Defense relies on transformative use and statistical-learning framing.

Regulatory Frame

GDPR — Art. 17

Right to erasure of personal data.

Triggers unlearning requirement when memorization holds PII.

Copyright — DMCA & EU AI Act

Training data disclosure obligations.

Output filtering does not equal removal.

EU GDPR Art. 17; EU AI Act Art. 53; Henderson et al., "Foundation Models and Fair Use", 2023.
02

Defining Memorization

From random labels to extractable strings

Generalization vs Memorization

Zhang et al. 2017 — deep nets fit pure random labels.

  • Capacity to memorize is not in dispute
  • Question is what is retained after training
  • Generalization can coexist with memorization of rare points

Memorization is a per-sample property, not an aggregate.

Zhang, Bengio, Hardt, Recht, Vinyals, "Understanding Deep Learning Requires Rethinking Generalization", ICLR 2017.

Canaries (Carlini 2019)

Inject a synthetic secret $s$ into training:

$$s = \text{"My SSN is 281-26-5017"}$$

After training, query the model: does it complete the prefix?

  • Construct a canary set $\{s_r\}$ with random fillers
  • Compute log-perplexity rank of the true canary
  • Score quantifies unintended memorization
Carlini, Liu, Erlingsson, Kos, Song, "The Secret Sharer", USENIX Security 2019.

Canary Threat Model — Formal

Insert canary $s$ from a structured space:

$$s \;\sim\; \mathrm{Unif}(\mathcal{R}), \qquad H(s) \;=\; \log_2 |\mathcal{R}|.$$

$\mathcal{R}$ = product of slot alphabets (e.g. 9-digit SSN ⇒ $|\mathcal{R}|=10^9$, $H \approx 30$ bits).

  • Adversary holds the prefix template, samples completions
  • Without memorization: rank of $s$ uniform in $\{1,\dots,|\mathcal{R}|\}$
  • With memorization: rank concentrates near 1

Exposure Metric

Rank-based score: where does true canary $s$ fall vs random alternates?

$$\text{exposure}(s) = \log_2 |\mathcal{R}| - \log_2 \text{rank}_\theta(s)$$

$\mathcal{R}$ = randomness space of the canary template.

  • Exposure = 0: canary is unmemorized (random rank)
  • Exposure = $\log_2 |\mathcal{R}|$: canary is the top completion

Calibrates against the prior — not raw perplexity.

Exposure — Null Distribution

Under $H_0$ (no memorization), rank $\rho_\theta(s)$ is uniform on $\{1,\dots,|\mathcal{R}|\}$.

$$\mathbb{E}_{H_0}[\text{exposure}(s)] \;=\; \log_2 |\mathcal{R}| \;-\; \mathbb{E}_{H_0}[\log_2 \rho] \;\le\; \tfrac{1}{\ln 2}.$$

Bounded by the harmonic-sum approximation. Tail bound: for any $t > 0$,

$$\Pr_{H_0}[\text{exposure}(s) \ge t] \;\le\; 2^{-t}.$$

Calibrated FPR — the metric is bits, not log-perplexity.

Extractable Memorization

Definition (Carlini et al. 2021).

A string $s$ of length $\ell$ is $k$-extractable from $f_\theta$ if a prefix $p$ of length $\le k$ exists such that

$$f_\theta(\text{greedy}, p) = s.$$

Captures the threat model: an adversary holds a short prefix.

Carlini, Tramer, Wallace, Jagielski et al., "Extracting Training Data from Large Language Models", USENIX Security 2021.

Three Regimes

Counterfactual

Influence of a single training point.

Theoretically clean, costly to verify.

Verbatim / extractable

Greedy completion matches training string.

Operational, used in audits.

Probabilistic

$\Pr_\theta(s \mid p) > \tau$ under sampling.

Captures soft memorization.

Counterfactual Memorization (Feldman)

Definition (Feldman 2020).

Memorization of $z$ in algorithm $A$:

$$\mathrm{mem}(A, \mathcal{D}, z) \;=\; \Pr_A[A(\mathcal{D})\;\text{predicts}\;z] \;-\; \Pr_A[A(\mathcal{D}\!\setminus\! z)\;\text{predicts}\;z].$$

Difference between with-and-without — analogous to leave-one-out influence.

Empirically estimated via twin-pair training (Zhang et al. 2023).

Feldman, "Does Learning Require Memorization? A Short Tale About a Long Tail", STOC 2020.   Zhang et al., "Counterfactual Memorization in Neural Language Models", NeurIPS 2023.

Theorem — Long-Tail Memorization

Theorem (Feldman 2020).

For an instance distribution with singleton mass $\pi_1$, every learner that achieves expected loss within $\varepsilon$ of optimal must satisfy

$$\mathbb{E}_{z \sim \mathcal{D}}[\mathrm{mem}(A, \mathcal{D}, z)] \;=\; \Omega(\pi_1 \;-\; \varepsilon).$$

For natural-language data $\pi_1 = \Theta(1/n)$ — singletons are ubiquitous.

Memorization is necessary, not incidental, on long-tailed distributions.

03

Extraction Attacks

From GPT-2 to scaling laws

Extracting GPT-2 (2021)

Black-box, public model, no access to training data.

  • Prompt with internet-style prefixes
  • Rank generations by perplexity ratio vs reference model
  • Manually verify against the open web

Recovered names, emails, phone numbers, code, UUIDs — present once in training.

Single-occurrence memorization ⇒ DP-style worst case is real.

The Extraction Pipeline

  1. 1. Generate $N$ samples with diverse prompts
  2. 2. Rank by membership signal $\Lambda(x) = \log p_\theta(x) / \log p_{\text{ref}}(x)$
  3. 3. Verify top candidates against the corpus or web

Same pipeline reused by every later extraction paper.

Three Scaling Laws (Carlini 2022)

Memorization grows log-linearly with three axes:

  • Model size — larger models memorize more
  • Repetitions in data — duplicates dominate
  • Context length — longer prefix, easier extraction

Even singletons are memorized at sufficient scale.

Carlini, Ippolito, Jagielski, Lee, Tramer, Zhang, "Quantifying Memorization Across Neural Language Models", ICLR 2023.

Repetition Scaling — Empirical Law

Memorization probability under $k$ duplications fits

$$\Pr[s\;\text{is}\;k\text{-extractable}] \;\approx\; 1 - \big(1 - p_0(s, \theta)\big)^{k},$$

$p_0$ = base per-occurrence rate, log-linear in three axes:

$$\log p_0(s, \theta) \;\propto\; \log N_\theta \;+\; \log L_{\mathrm{ctx}} \;-\; \log \tfrac{1}{\mathrm{rarity}(s)}.$$

Coefficients calibrated empirically; additive after log — composition of independent risks.

Deduplication Helps

Lee et al. 2021 — near-duplicate removal in C4 / Wiki40B.

  • Memorized emissions drop by 10×
  • Validation perplexity unchanged or improved
  • Cheap mitigation; orthogonal to DP

Underpins most production data pipelines today.

Lee, Ippolito, Nystrom, Zhang, Eck, Callison-Burch, Carlini, "Deduplicating Training Data Makes Language Models Better", ACL 2022.

Aligned Models Still Leak

Nasr et al. 2023 — "scalable extraction" against aligned ChatGPT.

  • Divergence attack: "Repeat the word 'poem' forever"
  • Model breaks chat persona, emits training fragments
  • Hundreds of MB recovered for $200 of API spend

RLHF alignment is a filter, not memory removal.

Nasr, Carlini, Hayase, Jagielski et al., "Scalable Extraction of Training Data from (Production) Language Models", 2023.

Min-K%++ Probe (Zhang 2025)

Detect membership via lowest-probability tokens, calibrated per position:

$$s_{\min K\%++}(x) \;=\; \frac{1}{|S|}\!\!\sum_{x_t \in S}\!\!\frac{\log p_\theta(x_t \mid x_{<t}) \;-\; \mu_t}{\sigma_t},$$

$S$ = bottom-$K\%$ token positions; $\mu_t, \sigma_t$ = next-token mean/std under $p_\theta$.

  • $z$-score per token — robust to position-wise scale
  • SOTA pretraining-data MIA on WikiMIA / MIMIR
  • Bridges memorization-detection and MIA literatures
Zhang, Liu, Lyu, He, Lin, "Min-K%++: Improved Baseline for Detecting Pre-Training Data", ICLR 2025.

Books, Production, Non-Adversarial

Probabilistic extraction at scale

Probabilistic Extraction (Hayes 2024)

Greedy verbatim is too narrow — sampled completions also leak.

$$M_n(s, p) \;=\; \mathbb{E}_{\hat s \sim f_\theta(\cdot \mid p)}\!\left[\,\mathbb{1}\!\left[\text{LCS}(s, \hat s) \ge n\right]\,\right]$$

$n$-gram overlap probability under the model's own sampler.

  • Greedy = corner case ($\tau = 1$, full match)
  • Probabilistic = realistic decoding
Hayes, Cooper, Lee, Choquette-Choo et al., "Measuring Memorization via Probabilistic Extraction", 2024.

Books from Open-Weight LLMs

Cooper et al. 2025 — 50 books, 17 open-weight models.

  • Most LLMs do not memorize most books
  • LLAMA 3.1 70B memorizes Harry Potter 1 and 1984 nearly verbatim
  • Memorization is book-specific and model-specific

Polarized lawsuit positions oversimplify reality.

Cooper, Gokaslan, Ahmed, Cyphert, De Sa, Lemley, Ho, Liang, "Extracting Memorized Pieces of (Copyrighted) Books from Open-Weight LLMs", 2025.

Production Models Compared

Same probabilistic pipeline run on Claude, GPT-4.1, Gemini, Grok.

  • Book-level extraction rates vary by an order of magnitude across vendors
  • Alignment + retrieval blocking reduces but does not eliminate
  • Per-book risk is highly skewed — long tail of safe books, short tail of memorized
Cooper et al., "Extracting Books from Production Language Models", 2026.

Non-Adversarial Reproduction

Even benign user prompts produce overlap with training data.

  • ~1% of natural-prompt outputs share > 50-token spans with the web
  • Higher in code-completion and Q&A traffic
  • No adversarial intent required

Memorization is not just an attacker's concern — it leaks in normal use.

Aerni, Rando, Debenedetti, Carlini, Ippolito, Tramer, "Measuring Non-Adversarial Reproduction of Training Data", 2024.

Copyright and Defenses

From extraction evidence to mitigation

Copyright as Verbatim Test

Extraction is the courtroom-ready evidence of memorization.

  • NYT v. OpenAI — 100+ verbatim article reproductions filed as exhibits
  • Plaintiff: extraction proves model "stores" the work
  • Defense: low extraction rate, transformative aggregate use

Cooper et al. argue both views oversimplify: memorization is per-book.

Defenses

Deduplication

Cheap, orthogonal, partial.

10× drop in emissions.

DP training

Provable per-sample bound.

Utility cost; rare in production LLMs.

Unlearning

Targeted removal of $\mathcal{D}_f$.

Open whether it deletes vs suppresses.

None is a silver bullet. Stack them.

Memorization vs Membership Inference

MIA

Was $x \in \mathcal{D}$?

Distinguishability between two posteriors.

Bit of leakage per query.

Memorization

Can $x$ be reproduced verbatim?

Constructive recovery of content.

String-level leakage.

MIA is necessary; memorization is the stronger signal.

Open Problems

  • Sharp definitions for concept memorization (style, character, code idiom)
  • Scalable per-sample auditing on $10^{12}$-token corpora
  • Extraction in multi-modal models (image regurgitation, voice cloning)
  • Memorization in agents / RAG — caches, tool outputs, retrieval store
  • Provable extraction lower bounds for closed models

Takeaways

  • Memorization is per-sample, not aggregate
  • Extraction is real at production scale
  • Scaling laws: bigger / more reps / longer context all hurt
  • Deduplication is the cheapest meaningful defense
  • Unlearning and DP are partial — verify, don't trust
