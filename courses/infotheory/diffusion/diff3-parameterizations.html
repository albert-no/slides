Diffusion Recap

The model we will analyze with three tools

The Forward-Reverse Machine

Forward (fixed): $x_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon$, $\;\varepsilon\sim\mathcal{N}(0,I)$.

Reverse (learned): $p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t) = \mathcal{N}(\mu_\theta(x_t,t),\,\tilde\beta_t I)$.

Hierarchical VAE: $T$ latents $(x_1,\ldots,x_T)$, frozen encoder, learned decoder.

Diffusion is the worked example; the tools are the subject.

Three Predictions, One Object

The master identity $x_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon$ can be solved for any of three targets:

$$\underbrace{x_0}_{\text{clean data}}, \qquad \underbrace{\varepsilon}_{\text{noise}}, \qquad \underbrace{\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t)}_{\text{score}}.$$

All equivalent up to $t$-dependent rescaling. Why? Tweedie (Section 03).

02

Tool 1: Variational Bound

ELBO gives training a tractable surrogate

Recall — ELBO Bounds the Intractable

Recall (Lecture 1). $\log p_\theta(x) = \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}} + D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q\,\Vert\,p_\theta(\cdot\mid x)) \;\ge\; \mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{ELBO}}.$

Likelihood intractable. Maximize the ELBO instead.

Gap vanishes iff variational posterior matches the true posterior.

Diffusion ELBO Decomposes

Plug the hierarchical VAE structure into the ELBO (Lecture 2):

$$-\log p_\theta(x_0) \;\le\; \underbrace{D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q(x_T\mid x_0)\,\Vert\,p(x_T))}_{L_T\,(\text{no }\theta)} + \sum_{t=2}^{T} L_{t-1} + \underbrace{(-\log p_\theta(x_0\mid x_1))}_{L_0\,(\text{small})}.$$

All training signal is in the interior terms $L_1,\ldots,L_{T-1}$.

Interior Term = MSE on the Mean

Both sides of $L_{t-1} = D_{\mathrm{KL}}\!\bigl(q(x_{t-1}\mid x_t,x_0)\,\Vert\,p_\theta(x_{t-1}\mid x_t)\bigr)$ are Gaussian, same covariance:

$$L_{t-1} \;=\; \frac{1}{2\tilde\beta_t}\,\|\tilde\mu_t(x_t, x_0) - \mu_\theta(x_t, t)\|^2.$$
Tool 1 alone reduces training to: predict the posterior mean $\tilde\mu_t$ from $(x_t, t)$.
03

Tool 2: Tweedie's Formula

Posterior mean of clean data = rescaled score

Tweedie's Formula

Theorem (Robbins, 1956 / Tweedie).

For $X_t = \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,X_0 + \sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\varepsilon$:

$$\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid X_t=x_t] \;=\; \frac{1}{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}}\bigl(x_t + (1-\bar\alpha_t)\,\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t)\bigr).$$

Posterior mean of clean $X_0$ = noisy $x_t$ + score correction, all rescaled.

Robbins, "An Empirical Bayes Approach to Statistics", Berkeley Symp. 1956.

Proof — Differentiate the Marginal

Marginal as Gaussian mixture; differentiate $\log p_t$:

$$\begin{aligned} p_t(x_t) &\;=\; \int p_0(x_0)\,\mathcal{N}\!\bigl(x_t;\,\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0,\,(1-\bar\alpha_t)I\bigr)\,dx_0. \\[6pt] \nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t) &\;=\; -\,\dfrac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid x_t]}{1-\bar\alpha_t}. \end{aligned}$$

Gradient passes inside; hits the Gaussian kernel. Solve for $\mathbb{E}[X_0\mid x_t]$. $\quad\square$

Tweedie Unifies the Predictions

Combine Tweedie with the master identity:

$$\mathbb{E}[\varepsilon\mid x_t] \;=\; -\sqrt{1-\bar\alpha_t}\,\nabla_{x_t}\log p_t(x_t).$$

Optimal $x_0$-predictor, $\varepsilon$-predictor, and score: three rescalings of the same function.

Tool 2 converts the ELBO's mean prediction (Tool 1) into a score estimation problem — bridging to Tool 3.

Tool 3: Fisher Divergence

The ELBO MSE is a score gap in disguise

Recall — Fisher Divergence

Recall (Lecture 6). $D_F(f\,\Vert\,g) = \tfrac{1}{2}\,\mathbb{E}_f\!\bigl[\|\nabla\log f - \nabla\log g\|^2\bigr].$

Compares scores, not densities. Ignores normalization constants.

Vincent (2011): minimizing Fisher divergence $\equiv$ denoising score matching.

Step 1 — Apply Tweedie to Both Means

Both the true mean $\tilde\mu_t$ and the model mean $\mu_\theta$ can be written as denoising estimates with variance $1-\bar\alpha_t$:

$$\begin{aligned} \tilde\mu_t &= x_t + (1-\bar\alpha_t)\,\nabla_{x_t}\log q(x_t\mid x_0), \\ \mu_\theta &= x_t + (1-\bar\alpha_t)\,\nabla_{x_t}\log p_\theta^{(t)}(x_t). \end{aligned}$$

Difference: a score gap scaled by $(1-\bar\alpha_t)$.

Step 2 — MSE Becomes Fisher

Substitute Step 1 into the per-step ELBO term:

$$\bigl\|\tilde\mu_t - \mu_\theta\bigr\|^2 \;=\; (1-\bar\alpha_t)^2\,\bigl\|\nabla_{x_t}\log q(x_t\mid x_0) - \nabla_{x_t}\log p_\theta^{(t)}(x_t)\bigr\|^2.$$

Average over $q(x_t\mid x_0)$:

$$\mathbb{E}_{q}\!\bigl[\|\tilde\mu_t - \mu_\theta\|^2\bigr] \;=\; 2\,(1-\bar\alpha_t)^2\;\underbrace{D_F\!\bigl(q(\,\cdot\,\mid x_0)\,\Vert\,p_\theta^{(t)}\bigr)}_{\text{Fisher divergence (Tool 3)}}.$$

Step 3 — Recognize the Denoising Form

Forward marginal $q(x_t\mid x_0) = \mathcal{N}(\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0,(1-\bar\alpha_t)I)$ has Gaussian score:

$$\nabla_{x_t}\log q(x_t\mid x_0) \;=\; -\,\frac{x_t - \sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,x_0}{1-\bar\alpha_t}.$$

Substitute, drop $\theta$-free terms: exactly Vincent's DSM at noise level $1-\bar\alpha_t$.

Vincent, "A Connection Between Score Matching and Denoising Autoencoders", Neural Comp. 2011.

Theorem — ELBO $\equiv$ Sum of DSM

Theorem (Vincent 2011; Song et al. 2021).

Diffusion ELBO maximization is equivalent (up to $\theta$-free constants) to minimizing

$$\sum_{t=2}^{T}\;\mathbb{E}_{X_0,X_t}\!\left[\tfrac{\log e}{2}\,\Bigl\|\tfrac{\sqrt{\bar\alpha_t}\,X_0 - X_t}{1-\bar\alpha_t} - s_\theta(X_t, t)\Bigr\|^2\right],$$

the sum of denoising score-matching objectives at noise level $1-\bar\alpha_t$.

Vincent, Neural Comp. 2011; Song et al., "Score-Based Generative Modeling", ICLR 2021.
05

Convergence

Three roads, one objective

Three Tools, One Loss

$$\begin{aligned} \text{ELBO MSE} \quad &\stackrel{(\text{Tweedie})}{=}\;(1-\bar\alpha_t)^2\,\bigl\|\nabla_{x_t}\log q(x_t\mid x_0) - \nabla_{x_t}\log p_\theta^{(t)}(x_t)\bigr\|^2 \\ &\stackrel{(\text{Fisher})}{=}\;\text{Fisher divergence at level } t \\ &\stackrel{(\text{DSM})}{=}\;\mathbb{E}\bigl\|\text{denoising direction} - s_\theta(x_t, t)\bigr\|^2. \end{aligned}$$
  • Tool 1 (ELBO): intractable likelihood $\to$ tractable MSE surrogate.
  • Tool 2 (Tweedie): MSE on means $\to$ MSE on scores.
  • Tool 3 (Fisher / DSM): score MSE $\to$ denoising regression.

The Information-Theoretic Arc

Three independently developed techniques, each from a different era:

  • Variational bounds (Jordan et al., 1999): approximate intractable posteriors.
  • Tweedie's formula (Robbins, 1956): posterior mean from the marginal score.
  • Fisher divergence (Hyvärinen, 2005): score-based training without normalization.
Diffusion is the model where all three converge to the same $\varepsilon$-prediction loss.

Recap

  • Diffusion: hierarchical VAE; three equivalent parameterizations ($x_0$, $\varepsilon$, score).
  • Tool 1 (ELBO): variational bound $\to$ MSE on posterior mean.
  • Tool 2 (Tweedie): posterior mean $=$ score, rescaled; bridges ELBO to Fisher.
  • Tool 3 (Fisher): per-step ELBO MSE $=$ Fisher divergence $=$ denoising score matching.
  • Convergence: three information-theoretic tools $\to$ one $\varepsilon$-prediction loss.
