KL-Differential Privacy

DP as a distributional similarity.

From Pure DP to Distributional Similarity

Pure / approximate DP bound

$$\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in S] \;\le\; e^\varepsilon \Pr[\mathcal{M}(X') \in S] \;(+ \delta)$$

for all events $S$. A natural alternative: compare the distributions of $\mathcal{M}(X), \mathcal{M}(X')$ directly via a divergence.

Definition (KL-DP).

$\mathcal{M}$ is $\varepsilon$ KL-DP if $D(\mathcal{M}(X) \| \mathcal{M}(X')) \le \varepsilon$ for all neighboring $X, X'$.

02

Rényi Divergence

A one-parameter family interpolating KL and $\ell_\infty$.

Rényi Divergence

Definition.

For $\alpha > 1$ and distributions $P, Q$,

$$D_\alpha(P \| Q) \;=\; \frac{1}{\alpha - 1} \ln \mathbb{E}_{x \sim Q}\!\left[\Bigl(\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}\Bigr)^\alpha\right].$$

$D_\alpha$ is non-negative; $D_\alpha(P \| P) = 0$. Larger $\alpha$ ⇒ more weight on rare-but-large likelihood-ratio events.

Limiting Cases

$\alpha \to 1^+$

$D_\alpha(P \| Q) \to D(P \| Q)$, the KL divergence. By convention $D_1 = D$.

$\alpha \to \infty$

$D_\alpha(P \| Q) \to \max_x \log \tfrac{P(x)}{Q(x)}$, the worst-case log-likelihood ratio — i.e. pure DP.

RDP at high $\alpha$ recovers pure DP; at $\alpha = 1$ it becomes the average-case KL bound.
03

Rényi DP & Properties

A family that composes additively.

Rényi Differential Privacy

Definition (Mironov 2017).

$\mathcal{M}$ satisfies $(\alpha, \varepsilon)$-RDP if for all neighboring $X, X'$,

$$D_\alpha\bigl(\mathcal{M}(X) \,\big\|\, \mathcal{M}(X')\bigr) \;\le\; \varepsilon.$$

Indexed by Rényi order $\alpha$; small $\alpha$ ≈ KL, large $\alpha$ ≈ pure DP.

Mironov, "Rényi Differential Privacy", CSF 2017.

RDP — Additive Composition

Composition.

If $\mathcal{M}_1$ is $(\alpha, \varepsilon_1)$-RDP and $\mathcal{M}_2$ is $(\alpha, \varepsilon_2)$-RDP (adaptive), then $(\mathcal{M}_1, \mathcal{M}_2)$ is $(\alpha, \varepsilon_1 + \varepsilon_2)$-RDP.

  • Composition is linear in $\varepsilon$ at a fixed $\alpha$ — no extra $\delta$, no $\sqrt{2k\ln(1/\delta')}$ term.
  • To get an $(\varepsilon, \delta)$ bound after $k$ steps, compose first at order $\alpha$, then convert.
RDP is the cleanest accounting tool for iterative mechanisms (DP-SGD, PATE).

Conversion: RDP ⇒ $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP

Theorem.

If $\mathcal{M}$ is $(\alpha, \varepsilon)$-RDP, then for any $0 < \delta < 1$,

$$\mathcal{M} \text{ is } \Bigl(\varepsilon + \tfrac{\ln(1/\delta)}{\alpha - 1},\; \delta\Bigr)\text{-DP}.$$

Standard Markov / Chernoff bound on the $\alpha$-moment of the privacy-loss RV. Optimize over $\alpha$ to get the tightest $(\varepsilon, \delta)$ trade-off.

  • Example: $(2, \varepsilon)$-RDP with $\delta = e^{-m}$ ⇒ $(\varepsilon + m,\; e^{-m})$-DP.

Lemma — $\infty$-Divergence Tames All Orders

Lemma.

If $D_\infty(P \| Q) \le \varepsilon$ and $D_\infty(Q \| P) \le \varepsilon$, then for $\alpha \ge 0$,

$$D_\alpha(P \| Q) \;\le\; 2 \alpha\, \varepsilon^2.$$

$\varepsilon$-DP (a worst-case bound) implies an average-case Rényi bound that scales as $\alpha\varepsilon^2$ — the key inequality for tight composition.

Advanced Composition via RDP

Composing $k$ $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP mechanisms:

  1. Each one is $(\alpha, 2\alpha \varepsilon^2)$-RDP by the lemma.
  2. By additive RDP composition, total is $(\alpha, 2\alpha k \varepsilon^2)$-RDP.
  3. Set $2\alpha k \varepsilon^2 \approx 2(\sqrt k \varepsilon)^2 \cdot \alpha$ — the $\sqrt k$ scaling pops out.

Converting back:

$$\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in S] \;\le\; \exp\!\Bigl(2\varepsilon \sqrt{k\, \log(1/\Pr[\mathcal{M}(X) \in S])}\Bigr) \Pr[\mathcal{M}(X') \in S].$$

This yields the advanced-composition $\sqrt k\,\varepsilon$ growth from Lecture 5.

Gaussian via RDP

A closed-form RDP guarantee for the Gaussian mechanism.

Gaussian Mechanism — RDP

Setup: $f : \mathcal{X}^n \to \mathbb{R}^d$ with $\ell_2$-sensitivity $\Delta$; $\mathcal{M}(X) = f(X) + \mathcal{N}(0, \sigma^2 I)$.

Theorem (Mironov 2017).

For all $\alpha > 1$, the Gaussian mechanism is $(\alpha, \varepsilon(\alpha))$-RDP with

$$\varepsilon(\alpha) \;=\; \frac{\alpha\, \Delta^2}{2\, \sigma^2}.$$

RDP $\varepsilon$ is linear in $\alpha$ — composition over $k$ steps just multiplies by $k$.

From Gaussian RDP to $(\varepsilon, \delta)$-DP

Using $\varepsilon = \varepsilon(\alpha) + \tfrac{\log(1/\delta)}{\alpha - 1}$:

$$\varepsilon \;=\; \frac{\alpha\, \Delta^2}{2\, \sigma^2} \;+\; \frac{\log(1/\delta)}{\alpha - 1}.$$

Optimizing over $\alpha$:

$$\sigma \;\ge\; \frac{\Delta}{\varepsilon}\sqrt{2 \log(1.25/\delta)}.$$

This recovers exactly the classic Gaussian-mechanism guarantee from Lecture 4 — the $1.25$ constant absorbs the optimization slack.

Proof — Step 1: Set Up the Integral

For $Y \sim \mathcal{N}(f(X'), \sigma^2 I)$,

$$D_\alpha(\mathcal{M}(X) \| \mathcal{M}(X')) = \tfrac{1}{\alpha - 1} \log \int f_{\mathcal{M}(X)}(y)^\alpha\, f_{\mathcal{M}(X')}(y)^{1-\alpha}\, dy.$$

Substituting the Gaussian densities ($\sigma^2 I$ covariance, means $f(X)$ and $f(X')$):

$$= \tfrac{1}{\alpha-1}\log \int \tfrac{1}{\sqrt{(2\pi\sigma^2)^d}}\, \exp\!\Bigl(-\tfrac{\alpha\|y - f(X)\|^2 + (1-\alpha)\|y - f(X')\|^2}{2\sigma^2}\Bigr)\, dy.$$

Proof — Step 2: Complete the Square

Inside the exponent, separate the quadratic-in-$y$ part from the constant:

$$\alpha\|y - f(X)\|^2 + (1-\alpha)\|y - f(X')\|^2 = \|y\|^2 - 2\langle y, \alpha f(X) + (1-\alpha) f(X')\rangle + \alpha\|f(X)\|^2 + (1-\alpha)\|f(X')\|^2.$$

Integrating the Gaussian over $y$ contributes $\exp\!\bigl(\tfrac{1}{2\sigma^2}\|\alpha f(X) + (1-\alpha) f(X')\|^2\bigr)$.

Proof — Step 3: Algebra

Combining:

$$D_\alpha(\mathcal{M}(X)\|\mathcal{M}(X')) = \tfrac{1}{\alpha - 1}\cdot \tfrac{\alpha(\alpha-1)}{2\sigma^2}\|f(X) - f(X')\|^2 = \tfrac{\alpha\,\|f(X) - f(X')\|^2}{2\sigma^2}.$$

Bounding $\|f(X) - f(X')\|_2 \le \Delta$:

$$D_\alpha \;\le\; \frac{\alpha\, \Delta^2}{2\sigma^2}. \;\blacksquare$$

Recap

  • KL-DP: $D(\mathcal{M}(X) \| \mathcal{M}(X')) \le \varepsilon$ — distributional similarity, but composes poorly alone.
  • Rényi DP $(\alpha, \varepsilon)$: bounds $D_\alpha$. Composition is additive in $\varepsilon$ at fixed $\alpha$.
  • Conversion: $(\alpha, \varepsilon)$-RDP ⇒ $(\varepsilon + \tfrac{\ln(1/\delta)}{\alpha - 1},\, \delta)$-DP; optimize $\alpha$.
  • Gaussian mechanism: closed-form $(\alpha,\, \tfrac{\alpha\Delta^2}{2\sigma^2})$-RDP — the engine behind tight DP-SGD accounting.
