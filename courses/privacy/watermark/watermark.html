01

Why Watermark Text

Provenance, design goals, threat models

The Central Question

Given text $x$ and a secret key $k$:

Was $x$ produced by a watermarked LLM?

Detector returns yes / no with calibrated false-positive rate.

No model access, no prompt — just the output.

Use Cases

Provenance

Detect AI-written essays, news, reviews.

Disinformation

Audit at-scale generated propaganda.

Data hygiene

Filter AI text from next-generation training corpora.

Detection beats classification — provenance is cryptographic, not statistical.

Three Design Goals

  • Detectability — short text, high TPR, low FPR
  • Quality — output distribution close to vanilla LLM
  • Robustness — survives editing, paraphrase, translation

Pick any two — every scheme makes a tradeoff.

The taxonomy below is organized by which two each method optimizes.

Adversary Model

Removal attacks

User edits, paraphrases, translates.

Goal: pass detector with high probability.

Spoofing attacks

Forge watermark on human text.

Goal: frame the model for content it didn't write.

Both matter. Spoofing is often under-evaluated.

02

Green-List Watermark

Kirchenbauer et al. 2023 — the workhorse method

Setup

At each decoding step $t$ with context $x_{<t}$:

  • Hash $x_{t-h:t-1}$ with key $k$ to seed a PRG
  • Partition vocabulary $\mathcal{V}$ into green $G_t$ ($\gamma|\mathcal{V}|$) and red $R_t$
  • Add bias $\delta > 0$ to green logits before softmax

Default: $\gamma = 0.25$, $\delta = 2$.

Kirchenbauer, Geiping, Wen, Katz, Miers, Goldstein, "A Watermark for Large Language Models", ICML 2023.

Watermarked Sampling

$$\hat p_\theta(v \mid x_{<t}) \;=\; \frac{\exp\!\big(\ell_t(v) + \delta\,\mathbb{1}[v \in G_t]\big)}{\sum_{w} \exp\!\big(\ell_t(w) + \delta\,\mathbb{1}[w \in G_t]\big)}$$

$\ell_t(v)$ are vanilla logits; bias only on green tokens.

  • High-entropy steps: green tokens get sampled with elevated probability
  • Low-entropy steps: bias swamped — quality preserved at "obvious" tokens

Detection: One-Proportion z-Test

Given $T$ tokens, count green hits $|s|_G$. Under $H_0$ (no watermark):

$$z \;=\; \frac{|s|_G - \gamma T}{\sqrt{T \gamma (1-\gamma)}} \;\sim\; \mathcal{N}(0, 1).$$

Reject when $z > z_\alpha$. False-positive rate is exactly $\alpha$ under $H_0$.

  • $T = 25$ tokens already gives $z > 4$ for typical generations
  • No model access at detection time — only key $k$

Detection Power — Entropy Bound

Under watermarked alternative $H_1$:

$$\mathbb{E}_{H_1}\!\big[|s|_G\big] \;\ge\; T\!\left(\gamma \;+\; \frac{(1-\gamma)\,(e^\delta - 1)}{e^\delta + 1}\,\bar H\right),$$

$\bar H$ = average per-step spike entropy of $p_\theta$.

  • Detection power $\propto$ text entropy, not length alone
  • Low-entropy steps (boilerplate, code) waste the bias budget
  • Tokens needed: $T \;\gtrsim\; \big(z_\alpha + z_\beta\big)^2 / \mathrm{var}_{H_1}(|s|_G/T)$

Information-theoretic ceiling: text with $\bar H = 0$ cannot be watermarked.

Quality–Detectability Tradeoff

Larger $\delta$

Stronger detection, fewer tokens needed.

Distribution shift away from $p_\theta$.

Smaller $\gamma$

Sharper z-test under $H_0$.

Smaller green list ⇒ more vocabulary distortion.

Distortion is per-token: KL divergence from vanilla decoder.

Open question for next section: can we get $\delta > 0$ without distortion?

03

Distortion-Free and Undetectable

Cryptographic watermarks that preserve $p_\theta$

Recall — Gumbel Distribution

$G \sim \mathrm{Gumbel}(0)$ has density and equivalent construction

$$f_G(g) \;=\; \exp\!\big(-g - e^{-g}\big), \qquad G \;=\; -\log(-\log U), \;\; U \sim \mathrm{Unif}(0,1).$$
  • $\mathbb{E}[G] = \gamma_E \approx 0.577$ (Euler–Mascheroni)
  • CDF: $F_G(g) = \exp(-e^{-g})$ — double-exponential tail
  • Stable under maximization of independent shifts

Building block for the next theorem and for Aaronson's scheme.

Theorem — Gumbel-Max Trick

Theorem.

Let $\ell_v = \log p_\theta(v)$ for $v \in \mathcal{V}$ and let $G_v \stackrel{\text{iid}}{\sim} \mathrm{Gumbel}(0)$. Then

$$\arg\max_{v}\;(\ell_v + G_v) \;\sim\; p_\theta.$$

Sampling from softmax = adding Gumbel noise to logits + arg-max.

Replace the $G_v$ with key-derived noise ⇒ derandomized sampling whose marginal still matches $p_\theta$.

Proof — Gumbel-Max

Compute $\Pr[\arg\max_w (\ell_w + G_w) = v]$. Condition on $\ell_v + G_v = m$:

$$\Pr[\forall w \ne v:\;\ell_w + G_w < m] \;=\; \prod_{w \ne v} F_G(m - \ell_w) \;=\; \exp\!\Big(\!-\!\sum_{w \ne v}\! e^{-(m - \ell_w)}\Big).$$

Integrate against $f_G(m - \ell_v)$ in $m$. Substitute $u = e^{-m}\sum_w e^{\ell_w}$:

$$\Pr[\arg\max = v] \;=\; \frac{e^{\ell_v}}{\sum_{w} e^{\ell_w}} \;=\; p_\theta(v). \qquad \blacksquare$$

Distortion-Free Watermark

Definition.

A watermark is distortion-free if, marginalized over key $k$, the output distribution equals the original LLM:

$$\mathbb{E}_k\!\left[\hat p_\theta(\cdot \mid x_{<t}, k)\right] \;=\; p_\theta(\cdot \mid x_{<t}).$$

Quality preserved on average; detector still succeeds with key.

Aaronson — Gumbel Trick

For each step, draw key-seeded uniforms $r_v \sim U[0,1]$ and pick

$$x_t \;=\; \arg\max_v \; r_v^{1/p_\theta(v)}.$$

Equivalent to Gumbel sampling — exactly follows $p_\theta$.

  • Detector recomputes $r_v$ from key, sums $-\log(1 - r_{x_t})$
  • Under $H_0$: $\sim \text{Exp}(1)$ per token
  • Under watermark: shifted toward small values

Aaronson Is Distortion-Free

Note that $-\log r_v \sim \mathrm{Exp}(1)$, hence

$$-\log(-\log r_v) \;\sim\; \mathrm{Gumbel}(0).$$

The Aaronson rule rewrites as

$$\arg\max_v \, r_v^{1/p_\theta(v)} \;=\; \arg\max_v\,\big(\log p_\theta(v) - \log(-\log r_v)\big) \;=\; \arg\max_v\,(\ell_v + G_v).$$

By the Gumbel-max theorem, marginal over key gives $x_t \sim p_\theta$. Distortion-free. $\blacksquare$

Kuditipudi: Provably Distortion-Free

Build watermark via inverse-transform sampling on a key-driven sequence.

  • Detector uses edit-distance alignment between key sequence and text
  • Robust to insertions, deletions, paraphrase
  • Cost: longer text needed, more compute at detection

First scheme that is provably distortion-free and robust to edits.

Kuditipudi, Thickstun, Hashimoto, Liang, "Robust Distortion-Free Watermarks for Language Models", TMLR 2024.

Theorem — Edit-Distance Robustness

Theorem (Kuditipudi et al. 2024).

Let $\tilde x$ be obtained from watermarked $x$ by Levenshtein edits of budget $\eta$. The aligned-key detector satisfies

$$\Pr_{H_1}\!\big[\,\text{detect}(\tilde x) = 1\,\big] \;\ge\; 1 - 2\exp\!\left(-\,c\,\frac{(T - 2\eta)^2}{T}\right).$$
  • Robust whenever $\eta = o(T)$ — paraphrase, deletion, insertion all bounded
  • Detection cost $O(T \log T)$ with dynamic-programming alignment
  • Tight up to constants in $c$

Unbiased Watermark

Hu et al. 2024 — formalize a family of unbiased schemes.

  • Token marginal preserved exactly (not just on average)
  • Includes Gumbel, inverse-transform, exponential mechanisms
  • Detection power scales with text entropy

Bridges Kirchenbauer-style biased and Aaronson-style cryptographic.

Hu, Chen, Lin, Goldstein, Huang, "Unbiased Watermark for Large Language Models", ICLR 2024.

Undetectable Watermarks

Definition (Christ, Gunn, Zamir 2023).

A watermark is undetectable if no PPT distinguisher without $k$ can tell watermarked from unwatermarked outputs with non-negligible advantage.

Cryptographic standard — based on PRFs and hardness assumptions.

  • Stronger than distortion-free
  • Embed via secret-key PRF on context hashes
  • Detection: with $k$, use same PRF to score
Christ, Gunn, Zamir, "Undetectable Watermarks for Language Models", COLT 2024.

CGZ Construction — PRF

Pseudo-random function $F_k:\{0,1\}^* \to [0,1]^{|\mathcal{V}|}$ replaces uniform randomness:

  • At step $t$, set $r_v = F_k(x_{<t}, v)$
  • Sample $x_t$ via inverse CDF on $p_\theta(\cdot \mid x_{<t})$ using $(r_v)$
  • Detection: recompute $F_k$, sum $\log p_\theta(x_t \mid x_{<t}) / \log r_{x_t}$ across positions
$$\text{score}(x) \;=\; \sum_{t=1}^T \log\!\frac{1}{1 - F_k(x_{<t}, x_t)}.$$

Without $k$: PRF is computationally indistinguishable from uniform ⇒ output distribution unchanged under any PPT distinguisher.

04

Robustness and Semantics

Surviving paraphrase and edits

The Paraphrase Attack

Pass watermarked text through a second LLM with rewrite prompt.

  • Token identities change ⇒ green-list signal weakens
  • Semantic content preserved
  • Cost: small budget; quality loss minimal

Vanilla Kirchenbauer at $\delta=2$: paraphrased text drops to $z \approx 1.5$.

Kirchenbauer et al., "On the Reliability of Watermarks for Large Language Models", 2023.

Provable Robust (Zhao et al.)

Use a fixed green list (no context hashing).

  • Loses local entropy but gains edit-distance stability
  • Detection power degrades smoothly with edit budget $\eta$
  • Theorem: TPR $\ge 1 - \exp(-c(T - \eta))$

Tradeoff: easier to spoof when key is reused.

Zhao, Ananth, Li, Wang, "Provable Robust Watermarking for AI-Generated Text", 2023.

Semantic-Invariant Watermark

Hash sentence embeddings, not token n-grams.

  • Synonym substitution leaves the embedding stable
  • Green list now indexed by semantic clusters
  • Recovers $z > 4$ after aggressive paraphrase

Embedding model becomes part of the trusted base.

Liu, Bu, Zhao, Yang et al., "A Semantic Invariant Robust Watermark for Large Language Models", ICLR 2024.

Permute-and-Flip Decoding

Replace softmax sampling with permute-and-flip rule.

  • Robust to adversarial decoding perturbations
  • Watermark embedded in the random permutation seed
  • Optimal under specific notions of regret + detectability
Zhao, Sezavar, Steinke, Su, "Permute-and-Flip: An Optimally Robust and Watermarkable Decoder for LLMs", 2024.

Adaptive Watermark (Liu 2024)

Apply bias only at high-entropy steps:

$$\delta_t \;=\; \delta \cdot \mathbb{1}\!\big[\,H\!\big(p_\theta(\cdot \mid x_{<t})\big) \;\ge\; \tau_H\,\big].$$
  • Skip low-entropy decisions where bias would hurt quality
  • Detector re-weights z-test by skip mask: $|s|_G - \gamma T_{\text{eff}}$
  • Pareto-improves Kirchenbauer at fixed quality
Liu, Bu, Zhao, Wei, Yan, "An Entropy-Based Adaptive Text Watermark for Large Language Models", ICML 2024.

WaterMax — Quality-First (Giboulot 2024)

Generate $N$ candidate continuations; pick the one maximizing detection score subject to quality threshold.

$$\hat x \;=\; \arg\max_{x \in \mathcal{C}_N(p)}\; \text{score}_k(x) \quad \text{s.t.} \quad \log p_\theta(x) \ge \tau_q.$$
  • Decouples quality and detectability — no logit bias on the path of generation
  • Cost: $N$-fold inference, but parallelizable
  • SOTA at low edit-budget regimes
Giboulot, Furon, "WaterMax: Breaking the LLM Watermark Detectability–Robustness–Quality Trade-off", NeurIPS 2024.
05

Beyond Standard LLMs

Radioactivity, production, diffusion-LLM

Radioactive Watermarks

Watermarked text is used to train a downstream LLM.

  • Statistical residue persists in the student's output distribution
  • Detect training-data contamination, not just direct generation
  • Audit pipeline: did model X train on watermarked outputs of Y?

Watermarks become a training-set tracer.

Sander, Yu, Faille, Furon, Jegou, "Watermarking Makes Language Models Radioactive", NeurIPS 2024.

SynthID-Text (Production)

Google DeepMind, Nature 2024 — deployed watermark for Gemini outputs.

  • Tournament-style sampling ⇒ approximately distortion-free
  • Live A/B over millions of users — quality unchanged in human eval
  • Open-source detector for non-adversarial provenance

Production watermarking is no longer hypothetical.

Dathathri, See, Ghaisas, Huang et al., "Scalable Watermarking for Identifying Large Language Model Outputs", Nature 2024.

Diffusion-LLM Watermarking (dgMARK)

Lab work: watermark for masked discrete diffusion LLMs.

  • AR token-by-token rule does not fit any-order unmasking
  • Embed signal at each remasking step, accumulate across passes
  • First scheme tailored to non-autoregressive decoding

See companion deck dllm/ for the dLLM substrate.

Hong and No, "dgMARK: Decoding-Guided Watermarking for Diffusion Language Models", 2026.

Open Problems

  • Adversarial-robust + distortion-free + short text — pick three?
  • Multi-key, multi-vendor detection — federated provenance
  • Watermark in code (low entropy) and structured outputs
  • Multi-modal: image + text + voice provenance under one key
  • Spoofing audits — public watermark verifier without key leak

Takeaways

  • Green-list = simple, deployed, paraphrase-fragile
  • Distortion-free schemes preserve $p_\theta$ on average
  • Undetectable = cryptographic gold standard, hardness-based
  • Robustness against paraphrase needs semantic / fixed-list tricks
  • Watermarks now trace training contamination, not just direct generation
